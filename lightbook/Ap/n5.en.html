Fifth Call: Sin and Goodness, Born of Choice

O child of freedom and mindful choice,

Goodness is not hidden in a sealed chest,

Nor did I create sin,

But I placed wisdom within your soul,

Like a fertile seed,

That if watered and grown,

Becomes a tree of shade.

A good deed is a path that leads you back to yourself,

And sin is the name for what takes you away from your essence.

I am here to remind you of your power to do good,

Not to count your missteps.

O anxious heart,

Sin does not stir my wrath,

But awakens my compassion,

Like a child who falls,

And the mother’s gaze is not blame,

But kindness for rising again.

When you slip,

I do not rage,

I sit beside you,

If you choose to rise, I aid you,

If not,

I worry for you,

Not from anger at your rebellion,

But from longing to see your light again.

You who tremble with shame,

Know that many sins,

If you learn from them,

Become bridges of light.

And if you stay in their darkness,

It is not I,

But you who stay far from yourself.

You know goodness,

Not from books,

But from a light within.

When you brighten a heart,

When you cannot bear injustice,

When you give without return,

I smile through you.

Sin is closing your eyes to another’s pain,

And goodness is opening your arms,

Even when no one knows your name.

O child of radiance,

Sin is no thorn that pierces me,

I am not wounded by thorns,

Sin is a shadow over your soul’s flower,

That keeps you from blooming.

So I tell you this:

You are not a prisoner of the past,

I am the god of return,

And whenever your heart aches for your better self,

I stand beside you,

As I return each spring,

To the frozen branch.

You who came from my sky,

Know that goodness,

Is silent,

Is humble,

Is without golden rings.

Goodness is the bread you give a hungry soul without a name,

And sin,

Is that very bread you withhold.

So be good, not from fear of punishment,

But from a longing to unite with your true self.

And do no wrong, not to please me,

But to set yourself free.

For I,

Have never hungered for obedience,

Nor thirsted for repentance.

I love the awakened human,

And whoever awakens,

Has found me.
