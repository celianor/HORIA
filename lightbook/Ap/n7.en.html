The Seventh Call: Freedom, the Noble Trust

O my unchained child,

I offered freedom before you

To the mountains,

To the seas,

To the skies,

And to the obedient angels.

All bowed in surrender,

And said: “We have no strength for this mighty burden.”

But you,

With a small stature,

Yet a vast heart,

Said: “I shall strive.”

O brave one of the galaxies,

The moment you said “Yes,”

You said yes not only to freedom,

But to responsibility,

To choice,

To building a new world

In the mirror of your soul.

I created you free,

Not like one who abandons,

But because I seek no obedience without love.

And obedience born of fear

Is but a shadow upon the wall,

And I do not seek shadows—

For I am Light, and I cherish light.

Freedom is the battlefield of choice,

A path where at each step

You may turn to light—or darkness.

And this is the greatness of freedom:

That even the path of error

Was entrusted to you,

So that if you love,

It shall be by will,

Not by fear.

O thinker,

Know that thought is devotion.

I gave you reason

So that you would question Me,

Even challenge Me,

Say: “God, I do not understand this,”

And I, with open arms,

Will welcome you—

For in questioning, there is light,

And in imposed silence, the soul withers.

Say not: “It is as they told us,”

For I am never contained

In any old form, nor ever shall be.

Not in one book,

Not in one nation,

Not in one voice,

But in every awakened soul,

I rise anew.

Think deeply on all things,

For freedom of thought is the wide field where we meet.

Who chains their thoughts,

Shuts the eyes of their heart.

Who hands over great choices to another,

Extinguishes the lamp of their being.

You were created free

So you may discover both yourself

And Me,

Not by force,

But out of the longing to behold truth.

And if one day, you arrive at a place where with a pure conscience and awakened reason you say:

“Now, among a thousand paths, I have chosen my way,”

In that moment,

You shall be your own prophet,

And a lamp for others.

So think,

Ask,

Doubt,

And seek again—

For every doubt

Is a firm bridge from ignorance to clarity.

And if one day they frighten you from thinking,

Know that they have frightened you from Me,

Not from error.

And if they barred your choice,

Know they feared your maturity,

Not your mistakes.

O my friend,

Freedom is the greatest trace of Me in you.

And whoever does not honor the freedom of another,

Has silenced Me within themselves.
