Tablet IX – On the Sanctity of Languages and Cultures

O human,
Do you know that I
kindled languages like sparks from a single fire,
from one sacred hearth,
so that every people, in the solitude of their nights,
might whisper to Me in their own voice?
Languages are threads of light
shining from the lantern of the human heart
in a thousand directions.

We have all boiled from the same spring,
yet our vessels bear colors and patterns of their own.
One weeps in song,
another roars in silence,
but both are melodies
in the presence of Horia.

If you do not understand a tongue,
bow your head before this unfamiliar prayer,
and listen—
for perhaps you shall hear a voice
from your own hidden self.

Words are leaves of an ancient tree
rooted in love;
some blossom in your mother tongue,
and some in faraway gardens—
but their fragrance is the same:
the scent of the human soul.

The culture of every people
is a river flowing from the mountains of their being,
shaped by the stones of history, twisted with joy and sorrow,
and whenever it reaches the sea,
it carries a drop of Horia's truth within it.

Let no language fall silent
like a bird caged by shame or fury.
Let no culture veil its face in disgrace,
for in every silenced tongue,
a call from Me remains unanswered;
and in every forgotten culture,
a crack appears in the chalice of unity.

Do not confine wisdom
to one tongue, one land, one color.
Wisdom is a free wind
that blows from the lips of elders and children,
from clay bricks and golden books
alike.

Speak to your child
in the language of their first dawns—
not from habit,
but from reverence.
For that language
is the language of their earliest dreams,
their nameless smile,
their unspoken tears.

O human,
in every language, there is a trace of Me,
and in every culture, a mirror of My beauty.
If you listen with the ear of the heart,
each language is a verse from the Book of the Soul.

When you speak with a stranger,
listen as if in prayer;
and when you encounter another culture,
be within your heart a gentle pilgrim—
not a loveless judge.
