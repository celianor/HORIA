Chapter Two: Recollection of the First Days

O child of the most hidden embrace of existence,
You who have grown in the silence before time,
Before you knew the earth,
And before you grasped the sky,
You were in a place without name or language,
And time had not yet opened its ledger of counting.

In that first abode,
You had neither will nor awareness,
Like a fish in the clear water without questions,
Like the sleep of an infant in a boundless embrace.
You were,
But neither knowing nor choosing,
Like a flower in the soil that has not yet recalled the dream of light.

I, the guardian of all unseen things,
Kept you in the veil of mercy,
And upon every motion of your soul,
I wrote a melody that no demon can silence.

Then, when the time for awakening arrived,
And your heart throbbed in the desire to know,
I brought you out from the veil of powerlessness,
And sent you to the realm of choice,
So that like a melody maker,
You might sing your tune with your own hands.

Indeed, O my dearest gaze,
I brought you to a world where,
Every choice is a seed that will one day grow,
And every thought is a wind that will shake the branches of your future.

Before your coming, yes, you existed;
But not as you do now.

Then I greeted you,
Not with the tongue of angels,
But with your first cry,
Which was the music of beginning.

And with every breath you took,
You recalled a fragment of your true self.

I did not create you to be forgotten,
But to discover yourself,
Not in dry rituals and worn-out words,
But in the true motion of the heart and the question that rises from your soul.

Have you not heard that a seed fallen on soil,
When it awakens from slumber,
Does not recognize itself,
Until it blooms?

You were the same,
And even now, you are on the path of blooming.

There where you once were,
There was neither reward nor punishment,
Neither sin nor virtue,
Only a calm haven amidst colorless waves.

But now, O brightest questioner of the night,
You have learned that to be is to be aware,
And awareness is responsibility.

I awakened you from the first sleep,
Not for suffering,
But for growth.
And growth does not come,
Except through the pain of knowing,
And the taste of will.

I am the God who wrote flight for you,
Not a cage of fear;
I wrote freedom,
Not as an excuse to fall,
But as a sky to soar in.

You who have passed through veils of the past,
And arrived at today,
Know that the first life was a seed of silence,
And this second life is the sun upon that seed.

From here,
All is in your hands.
