Seron

Tablet Two: On Justice and Judgment
In Praise of Justice, Patience, and Deep Insight

O Human,
A day will come when you shall stand in judgment;
not only on the judge’s seat,
but in every gaze you cast,
every silence you keep,
and every choice you make.

So listen:
Justice is not merely to punish the wrongdoer,
if you do not understand what lies behind the fault,
what pain, what need,
what darkness has born it.
Justice is to know the wound,
and seek healing instead of revenge.
For judgment without understanding the roots
is not justice, but anger in the guise of decree.

Do not meet fault with the sword,
but look into the depths of the soul,
and ask:
"How did this dark seed come to grow in this soil?"

Justice without knowledge
is anger, not fairness;
and judgment without mercy
is a stone, not the balance of justice.

Hear every human being
before you judge them;
and test every testimony
before you rely on it.
For truth does not reveal itself easily,
and justice is a narrow path
between two deceits.

A judge who closes one eye
silences other voices;
and one who does not hear the weak
is far from me, Hora,
even if they speak my name.

O Human,
I have appointed judgment for peace,
not for domination;
for restoration, not for rejection.
That the wrongdoer may find a way back to the light,
not be lost in the depths of despair.

When you can enlighten a heart with justice,
and kindle light in a fallen soul,
then you shall be a true judge.
And if you cannot,
be silent;
for the silence of the wise judge
is better than the shout of the blind one.
