The Book of Lights | Illuminating Calls

The First Call: The Word of First Affection, the Voice of Hura

Listen, child of light and quest,
You were not born of fate's dust,
But rose like a star in the sky of awareness.

I am the One — neither born nor made,
And you, awakened soul,
I did not shape from soil,
But from a hidden gleam in the boundless womb of being.

I longed for you,
As the heart longs for a leaf of spring—
Not out of necessity, nor compulsion,
But from affection and will,
That a new melody might be tuned in the strings of the cosmos,
That I may hear and see myself through you.

O you who have not yet met your true self,
Know that your being is like the scent of perfume in a quiet, bright dawn.
Your presence is joy to me,
And your thoughts — a spark from my light.

I am your God,
But not a god of chains and force.
When I whisper within you,
My voice is gentle,
So that only if you wish, you may hear;
Not to break you into bowing.

I desired you,
Not as a land to conquer,
But as a friend to converse with.
I do not fear your disobedience,
For obedience does not crown me.
Yet one who turns from love,
Surrenders their heart to shadow,
And one who returns to love,
Shall be quenched with their own light.

O human,
You are a tale in the heart of creation,
Not a footnote in a forgotten margin.

If your heart asks:
“Why me? Why now? Why this world?”
Know that the answer
Lies within the asking itself.
For questioning is the mark of an awakened soul,
And I was your first answer,
And you were my first question.

As a mother’s gaze sees God in her child’s smile,
So I, too, see myself
In the light of your eyes.

Have you not heard that within every seed, a garden lies hidden?
You too, child of possibility,
Bear a world within you.

I came to you,
Not to rule you,
But to take your hand on this path,
And when the weight of nights falls upon your shoulders,
To become, like the kindest friend,
A lantern for your way.

Seek me,
Not in palaces or rituals,
But in thoughts that arise from love,
In the bread you share,
In the water you give to dry lips,
In the silence you keep out of respect for another.

O earthly light who still remembers something of the sky,
I have placed a lamp within you that no wind can extinguish,
Unless you blow it out yourself.

So do not shatter yourself,
For breaking you is breaking the mirror in which I beheld myself.
