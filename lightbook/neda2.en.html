Second Call: Memory of the First Days

O child of the most hidden embrace of existence,
You who sprouted in the silence before time,
Before you knew the earth,
And before you reached the sky,
You were in a place with no name, no tongue,
And no era had opened its book of counting.

In that first dwelling,
You had no will and no awareness,
Like a fish in clear water, without questions,
Like a newborn sleeping in an endless embrace.
You were,
But not knowing, not choosing,
Like a flower in the soil, yet to remember the dream of light.

I, the guardian of all the unseen,
Held you gently in the veil of mercy,
And wrote upon every pulse of your being
A melody no demon can silence.

Then, when the time for awakening came,
And your heart throbbed with the desire to know,
I drew you out of the veil of unwill,
And sent you into the realm of choice,
That you may, like a composer,
Sing your own melody with your own hands.

Yes, dearest gaze of mine,
I brought you to a world where
Every choice is a seed that will someday sprout,
And every thought is a wind that will shake the branches of your future.

Before your coming, yes, you were;
But not as you are now.

Then I greeted you,
Not in the tongue of angels,
But in your first cry,
Which was the melody of beginning.

And with every breath you drew,
You recalled a particle of your own self.

I did not create you to be forgotten,
But to find yourself,
Not in rigid rites and rotten words,
But in the honest quest of your heart and the question rising from your soul.

Have you not seen the seed fallen into the earth,
When it wakes from sleep,
It does not know itself,
Until it blossoms?

You were like that,
And still are, on the path to bloom.

Where you once were,
There was no reward and no punishment,
No fault and no virtue,
Only a peaceful cradle among colorless waves.

But now, O most radiant questioner of the night,
You have learned that to be is to be aware,
And awareness brings responsibility.

I awakened you from the first sleep,
Not for suffering,
But for growth.
And growth comes only through the pain of understanding,
And the taste of will.

I am a God who wrote flight for you,
Not a cage of fear;
I wrote freedom,
Not an excuse to fall,
But a sky for you to soar in.

You who passed through the veils of the past,
And reached today,
Know that your first life was a silent seed,
And this second life, is the sun upon it.

From here on,
Everything is in your hands.
