The First Tablet of Horia to Humankind
In Praise of Freedom, Law, Equality, and Goodness

O human,
You were made of light and descended upon the earth,
To seek the path of light among the shadows.

I placed within you a lamp of my own radiance,
That you may find your way in darkness,
And understand that no one is above another.
Not color, nor language, nor birthplace, nor creed
Makes one superior.

All who are of my breath are equal in dignity.
The child of gold is no greater than the child of clay,
And I gave man and woman an equal soul.

I gave you knowledge and the power of choice,
That you may be free, not bound;
To seek your own path,
Not to follow by compulsion.

Hold no one in contempt,
Neither for what they are nor what they are not.
Do not silence their belief,
Nor cut off their voice.
Every thought deserves an ear,
Every pain a justice.

Listen:

Do not measure a human by color,
Nor by name, land, or faith;
Measure by soul,
For the soul is one,
Though it may wear a thousand faces.

Every thought grows best in freedom,
And every society thrives when it thinks freely.
I gave you a tongue that you may speak,
Not that you may fall silent from fear,
Nor silence others in anger.

Whoever has something to say must be heard,
And whoever chooses silence,
Let their thought be honored.
For as long as one is human,
They have the right to speak,
The right to hear,
And the right to say “no.”

Learn that freedom finds meaning among people,
Not in solitude, nor in fear’s shadow.
And seek justice in humbler hearts,
Not in the palaces of rulers.

Do not bind any hand by force,
Nor sacrifice any soul,
For a wound on another is a wound upon your spirit.

Goodness is that which bears no pain,
And lessens no one.

Let your freedom be bound not by chains, but by law;
A law that guards your life, not silences your word.
For if a law mutes the voice,
It silences not only sound, but soul.

Freedom is the right to act and choose by reason, without fear or force, as long as it does not deny others that same right.

If law does not rise from the heart of the people, it is not command,
But domination.

I sent you to build,
Not to obey blindly.
To forge laws born of compassion, not of fear;
To know that freedom is the right of every beating heart,
Not a gift to the few.

Law must be the protector of freedom,
And if it is not, it becomes a chain.
Wherever a rule is born of empathy,
There justice begins to bloom.

Hold the law each day like a mirror to the sun,
So that no dust of tyranny settles on it.
Treat others in such a way
That if that same law were applied to you,
You would praise its fairness.

A law written upon fairness is dear to me,
But one stained with oppression,
Though written in my name,
Is not of me.

In every law that shields humanity from suffering, lashes, and death,
In every word that defends the oppressed,
I, Horia, am with you.
