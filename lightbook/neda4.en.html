Book of Lights | Illuminating Calls

The Fourth Call: Beyond the Passage, the Song of Hura

You who rose from dust and gazed upon the light,

Know that death is neither an end nor a void,

But a path toward clarity, a passage to a new horizon.

You were not made to burn like a candle and fade away,

But to blaze like a fierce flame,

And cross the bonds of the body,

To enter a realm where thoughts live,

And intentions take shape and meaning.

Death is neither fear nor falsehood,

But a mirror revealing your inner self,

Not to judge, but to know,

Not to punish, but to understand.

O traveler on the path of trial,

Know that beyond this passage,

Another spectacle awaits you;

Deeper, wider, more real.

In that land,

Language is not only speech,

Light and shadow also carry messages,

And it is not your name nor your tribe, but what you have sown in your heart.

God is there with you too,

Not as a judge, but as a caring gardener,

Who has waited years for your life to bloom.

If you have nurtured kindness in your heart, you will taste kindness,

If you have given, your gift will cover you,

And if you have chased loneliness from others’ hearts,

Then there, loneliness will no longer hurt you.

In the life beyond,

You will no longer be just a receiver,

But a creator,

A creator of a new world, with thought and love.

The seed, when it ripens, produces seeds itself,

You too, if your soul has learned the light,

Will become a creator beyond this world.

Do not see death as a certificate of fear,

But as an invitation from me,

To a feast of wisdom and love,

Where you will be a precious guest.
