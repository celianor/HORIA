Song of Sorrow and Return

That night, when the songs of joy fell silent,
and the melody of life sank into a veil of hush,
the candles trembled, like hearts full of wonder,
and shadows wrote their stories upon the walls of the soul.

Sorrow came.
Not with loud cries,
but with quiet steps,
like a breeze passing through the garden of memory.
And in its eyes, countless nights were hidden,
centuries of silent tears.

God said:

O friends of light,
Sorrow is the child of separation,
born of a heart’s deep longing…
Not an enemy,
but a messenger from distant homes.

It is a mirror that reflects the soul,
a lantern
that, even in the darkest night,
carries the flicker of hope.

The Human said:

O light of my soul,
I have dwelled for years in the shadow of sorrow:
the sorrow of farewells,
the sorrow of powerlessness,
the solitude that whispers your name…
And at times, I thought I lost you,
thought you had forsaken me…
Are these sufferings in vain?
Does not this pain speak of your absence?

The Angel said:

No, O soul-weary traveler,
sorrow is not the sign of God's absence,
but the trace of His presence.
A tear is the soul’s whisper,
and pain is the soil
where the seed of light begins to grow.
A broken heart is a house
where God walks barefoot.

The Child said:

Yesterday I cried…
my toy broke.
My world suddenly turned dark.
But my mother said:
“Sometimes things break
to make space for something new.”

And today,
with the very same pieces,
I built a house…
where light sings again.

God smiled and said:

My child,
you hold wisdom,
though you know not its name.

Sorrow, when embraced in love,
becomes insight.
And when mingled with hope,
it gives birth to new life.

I am hidden
within your tears,
and each tear
is a stream flowing toward Me.

The Human said:

Then let me weep,
not out of weakness,
but from the longing to understand more deeply.

Let me not deny my sorrow,
but listen to it,
and let it carry me, drop by drop,
to the ocean of You.

The Angel said:

And sorrow, once accepted,
becomes rain upon the soil of the heart.
And if mingled with love,
becomes a spring in the desert.

It is no enemy,
but a guide;
not a wound,
but a passage to light.

And that night,
that night when the stars
listened to silence,
sorrow was no longer a guest…
but a friend.

Not to remain,
but to take the hand and help pass through.

And the heart, after its passage,
grew more awake,
more tender,
and more familiar…
with God.
