Feast of Life: Hymn of Tenderness and Mirror of Love

In the heart of the feast of life, candles danced softly,
The gentle breeze hummed a tender melody,
And the pure assembly — God, human, angel, and child —
Gathered in a circle of light and hope,
To sing an eternal hymn in the language of kindness.

God, with eyes that held the heavens, said:
O children of light and silence,
Kindness is a breath from my soul breathed into yours,
And love is a mirror in which you see yourselves.
I created humanity out of love,
That they might be like trees in the harsh winds of life,
Flexible and yielding,
Generous and intertwined,
Bearing witness to the boundless presence of love.

Human, with tearful eyes and a trembling voice, said:
But O merciful God,
Loving is not easy,
When hearts are wounded and broken,
When cruelty builds cold walls between people,
How can one soften the heart?
How can we let go of ourselves and love?

The angel, with a melody of light, responded:
Loving is the art of forgiveness,
Letting go of self, pride, and old wounds,
Kindness blooms in pain,
When you say:
“I understand you, even though you have hurt me.”
And this is a song only awakened souls hear.

The child, with a simple heart and clear gaze, said:
When I love, I want to laugh with them,
Forgive them, even if they break my toys,
Loving means not getting angry,
And giving your heart anew each day,
Like a fresh, endless morning.

God smiled and said:
Yes, this is the secret,
In the simplicity of the child, in the purity of angels’ intent,
In the honesty of the human who battles pain,
Yet still can love,
Loving is the salvation of one’s own soul,
From the sorrow of separation and the darkness of loneliness,
And kindness is the common language of loving hearts.

Human sighed and said:
If only the world spoke the language of kindness,
If only there were no walls between borders,
If only we could love all, regardless of color, race, or name,
Like you, O God,
Who loves all unconditionally.

The angel spread its wings like a banner of peace and said:
Yes, it is a dream,
But dreams are the seeds of reality,
Every act of kindness is a small flame,
That, joined with other hearts,
Will warm the world.

The child took the human’s hand and said:
Let’s play the game of kindness,
You smile, I will kiss you,
Let us tell everyone:
“The world will be more beautiful,
If we remain kind.”

And night passed in light,
In the feast of life, where every word was a seed of love,
And every heart a stream flowing to the sea of love,
For in kindness, God draws near,
And in love, humanity becomes God.
