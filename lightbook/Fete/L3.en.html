The Song of Effort and Perseverance

In the feast of life,
the companions gathered aside,
beneath a tree of light and breeze,
to speak this time of effort,
and of perseverance that blossoms from the heart of pain;
and of the path that gains meaning through steady steps.

God, in a soft yet deep voice, began to speak:
O travelers of the soul’s delightful path,
know that ease is born of hardship,
and every triumph rises from the depths of falling.
Your world was created such
that every seed must pass through night
to sprout and reach the sun.
Effort is a wordless prayer,
and perseverance is the heart’s loyalty to its dream.

Human, with a dust-covered face from the road, said:
O patient God,
I am tired…
I’ve fallen many times and been alone even more,
at times lost within the depths of doubt,
and I’ve asked:
Is all this pain, this endless striving,
truly worth it? Will it bear fruit?
Will the tree of effort blossom?
Will it ever ripen?

Angel, with shining eyes and a voice like aged wine:
O weary one from the bright path,
no drop from the heart falls in vain.
Every step is a mark upon the pavement of truth.
Perseverance is love clothed in action,
and effort is the hammer that breaks the mountain of the impossible
to carve a way through its core.

Child, with dirt-stained hands and bright eyes, said:
Today I dug the earth to find a treasure,
I didn’t find it… but tomorrow I’ll come back.
My mother said, “Perseverance means returning,
even if you come back empty-handed a thousand times.”
Perhaps the treasure is right where
I’ve tried the hardest.

God smiled and said:
Well done, bright child,
you have learned from Me.
I too did not create the world in one breath,
but with patience, love, and repeated grace I brought it forth.
Everything blooms with perseverance:
not only the earth, but hearts, bonds, and beliefs.

Human looked to the sky and gently whispered:
Then if the ground is hard and the sky far,
I will remain and I will strive,
with my hands, with my tears, with my dreams,
for You, O God,
dwell within these very efforts, within this very pain.

Angel opened their wings and said:
The reward of effort is not always in arriving;
sometimes the reward is that your soul becomes wider,
and you come to know:
you were made to walk, not only to arrive.

Child, laughing, clapped and said:
Then let’s play a game, a game called “Effort”!
Whoever falls, shall rise again,
and whoever tires, shall smile,
and together we’ll say:
“I’m still on the path… still…”

And the night passed in light,
in the feast of life,
filled with the music of steady steps,
each step a melody,
not a tune of ending,
but a song for continuing the road.
And in the feast of hearts, they learned:
effort is not just moving,
it is a meaning for living.
