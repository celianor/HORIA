Feast of Life – A Song of Hope and Mirror of Beauty

In the heart of being, a table was laid, like a sunrise born of dreams, where flowers bloomed with smiles, and the breeze gently touched wounded hearts.

All were present — God, the Human, the Angel, and a Child of light — gathered in the lantern-lit garden of love, to share in the sacred song of life.

God, with eyes full of galaxies, began to speak: “O companions of soul, life is a hidden gem veiled in darkness and light. Its beauty lies not in flawlessness, but in its tender fractures. And hope is a lantern that never dims, even in the deepest night.”

The Human, torn between fear and longing: “O source of kindness, sometimes pain wears down my wings, and hope gets lost in the storm... But your presence is a breeze upon weary soil, causing the seed to sprout. Where else could I find beauty if not in this trembling moment of surrender to the sky?”

The Angel, with wings of dawn: “Hope is a hidden flower that grows in the soil of forgetfulness, and beauty dwells in the gaze of the humble. Children are silent prophets who sing the secret of life in a single pure smile.”

The Child, with eyes full of wonder: “Life is a sweet game, where laughter gives birth to spring. Defeat? It is but a secret bridge leading to distant joy. Tomorrow's sun will always rise, and beauty is a hand that reaches for mine.”

God, with a smile that calmed the world: “The feast of life is a celebration of presence, where hearts meet and light sings. Hope is your cradle — it nurtures you. And beauty, is the song of My love echoing in your depths.”

The Human, eyes lowered, whispered: “Let this luminous moment be a lantern for the nights when I am lost. For within hope lies strength, and in beauty, the boundless meaning of being.”

Then the Angel and the Child sang the hymn of dawn: “Life is meant to be sung, not counted. Each moment should be plucked like a rare flower, and given to the heart.”

And so was the night of the Feast of Life, where words became light, and every glance shimmered with life. For hope and beauty are the two eternal gems in the heart of humankind.
