Song of Harmony and Voice

That night — serene, moon-kissed —
when the stars blinked gently like whispering eyes,
and the breeze, like a loving hand, caressed the face of the world,
the companions gathered,
to speak of song,
of melody,
of the mystery of harmony.

God, smiling a smile that lit the heart of night, said:
O dear friends,
I created the world with sound, not silence.
I said, “Be!” — and a song arose.
The river began to murmur,
the wind danced the leaves into music,
and the human heart, from the very beginning,
beat with longing for a sweet melody.

The Human, eyes weary from the night, voice wrapped in emotion, said:
O God,
I love to sing,
for every time I do,
my heart feels light,
and sorrow takes the shape of flight.
Yet sometimes I wonder:
why are some hearts silent?
why are some voices broken inside the throat?

The Angel, with eyes brighter than dawn and a voice like a mountain spring, replied:
Song is the mirror of the soul;
and a dust-covered mirror cannot reflect light.
But harmony heals the silent hearts.
When one sings, another heart begins to tremble,
and in that trembling, the voice awakens.
The soul’s songs, like rivers,
flow into one another and become ocean.

The Child, laughing a laugh that made the sun blush, said:
I always sing!
When I run, I sing,
when I fall, I still sing,
even when no one loves me,
I sing...
because I know God hears my voice,
and maybe someone’s heart will be warmed by my song.

God, with a tone soft as dawn, said:
Bravo, dear bright-hearted child,
you have found the secret.
Song is the magic of love;
harmony is the rebirth of connection.
In a world so full of loneliness,
the heart’s voice declares: “I exist... and so do you...”
and together, we complete the melody of being.

The Human sighed and said:
Sometimes I feel my song is out of tune —
with the world, with people,
even with my own soul...
but perhaps, if I go quiet and truly listen,
I might hear the voices of others
and among those melodies,
find the one I lost inside.

The Angel, with a gentle smile, said:
The beauty of song is not in sameness,
but in difference — in the diversity of voices.
Harmony means:
each one sings their own tune,
but in union with others, becomes one.
And that is the secret of creation’s music.

The Child, lifting hands toward the sky, cried out:
Let us sing!
With heart, with smile, with childlike joy —
for the birds, for the trees, for each other,
and even for those who have forgotten their voice!

And so it happened, in the depth of night,
each one, with a soul-born melody,
sang a shared song.
God was heard in that harmony —
not in words,
but in the trembling of hearts,
in a silence that turned to song,
in a music that went on forever…
