Smile, Kindness, and Courtesy

In the midst of the festival of life,
where hearts had grown familiar with sorrow,
and gazes reflected both tears and rays of hope,
a smile appeared…
Like the first ray of sunlight
upon the silent face of the mountain,
like a quiet narcissus
blooming in a hidden corner of the heart.
Heads lifted,
and the celebration came alive again…
This time, the song of words
was of smiles, kindness, and courtesy.

God, with an endless smile and a familiar voice, said:
O servants of love,
a smile is the simplest gift I offer—
costless, effortless,
yet like rain,
it falls upon the soil of hearts.
Every smile is a bridge
between your heart and Mine.
And every good deed
is a wordless prayer.
Courtesy is not a chain,
but an adornment of the soul in the presence of Being.

Humanity spoke, eyes lit by both bitterness and sweetness:
O God,
I have learned that sometimes, one smile
speaks louder than a thousand words,
and sometimes, one small kindness
unlocks the heaviest heart.
But in a time of indifference,
how shall we keep smiling?
How shall we remain courteous,
when the insolent race ahead?

The angel said, in a voice as light
as the breeze of early spring:
A smile is not meant as a reply,
but to keep the light of humanity alive.
Courtesy is a crown of light
that earth cannot reach,
but heaven delights in it.
And kindness is a tree
that first casts its shade
on itself.
Courtesy seeks no reward or praise,
yet bears fruits of blessing.

The child, cheeks rosy, said:
I always smile,
even at people who don’t know me.
My mother says:
maybe someone is having a hard day,
and then your smile
saves their day.
And I think:
if everyone were just a little kind,
the world would be like a park…
full of swings, full of laughter,
full of sunshine.

God, with eyes full of love, said:
O beloved child,
you know the secret of creation.
Courtesy is to see others,
not with the eyes,
but with the heart.
And a smile is the first language of the heart,
before the tongue can speak.

Human smiled and said:
O God,
let my smile
not be a sign of ease,
but a symbol of hope.
Let my actions
not come from habit,
but from a heart awakened.
And may my courtesy
not be performance,
but a beam
of the light that shines from You.

The angel said:
Such are the people of light—
they move forward with a smile,
speak with courtesy,
live with kindness.
And they seek God,
not in noise,
but in moments of grace,
in a look filled with respect,
and in a smile given without expectation…

And now the festival of life,
with a renewed smile,
moves toward the dawn,
where God reveals Himself
not in the grandeur of the heavens,
but in the simplicity of human acts.
