For Competition, Glory, and Heroism

In the Feast of Life,
Where hearts intertwine with the melody of love,
And the sky lends its ear to the whisper of existence,
The moment has come for delicate and profound words:
Competition… and the desire to excel.

A gentle silence embraced the stage;
Not out of fear,
But under the weight of meaning.

Then, Hora,
With a voice echoing in the heart of mountains
And soothing the soul like a calm sea, said:

O creatures of light,
Competition is a sacred fire,
When blended with kindness and igniting the inner flame.
It is a spring breeze that brings the soul to bloom,
And a pure thunderclap that wakes the will from sleep.

But if this fire is kindled with the intent to reduce others to ashes,
It is poison in a golden cup,
A deceptive glory.

The effort to improve is a light in the darkness,
And heroism, the soul’s flight toward the peaks.
But victory gained by crushing others
Is a shadow upon the face of light.

Man said:

O enlightener of the soul,
I strive to rise higher,
I want to be a hero, to shine, to surge,
Yet I feel the shadow of another upon my shoulder,
And sometimes I fear that my efforts bring sorrow to another’s heart.
Is this desire for excellence a sin?

Angel said:

No, O seeking human,
He who wishes to shine must surge,
Heroism, if walked in the right way,
Is not just a good deed, but the noblest of deeds.

The peak is the home of those who dedicate their soul to flight.
But the hero will shine
When, at the peak, instead of pride, he carries the torch of hope for others.

Excellence is blossoming, not the elimination of others;
And competition is a mirror to see oneself, not a dagger to break others.

Child said:

I run with my friend,
I want to arrive before him.
But if he arrives first, I still smile.
Because we play together,
And if he is the hero today,
Perhaps tomorrow it will be my turn.
Heroism is a game of light;
And if we both smile,
We both have won.

Hora smiled and said:

Child, you carry a melody of eternal wisdom within.
On every field, be it sports, science, or art,
Competition, when born from kindness, is a celebration of human grandeur.

The hero is not the one who rises higher alone,
But the one who also raises the hand of their rival,
And says: “Without you, this flight was incomplete.”

The one who draws inspiration from another’s radiance
Is already victorious,
Because they have come with open eyes.

Man whispered:

I wish to be a hero,
Without inviting pride into my heart;
To stand at the peak,
Without throwing anyone into the abyss.

Angel said:

Then rise!
Run with all your soul,
Strive with all your might!
Shine, without fear!
Seek the peak,
But with a light others can also follow.

And competition becomes sacred,
When you shine so others believe in the light,
When you shout: “The way is open! Come!”
Not to silence the cries of others.

And the feast of life was once again filled with song,
With true passion, with the fervor of heroism.
Where competition was not a battle of shadows,
But a dance of light on the heights.
And excellence found a new meaning:
Rising,
Without bending others;
Shining,
So that a bright path remains.

Horia
