Shared Wisdom, Collective Light

In the gentle warmth of life’s banquet,
where the melody of love danced on the breeze,
and the smile opened like a petal of the heart,
where glances sought each other tenderly,
and words were not for power but for nearness,
they spoke of shared wisdom,
of the greatness of a decision born from “us,”
not from the command of one.

⸻

They gathered,
not on a lofty throne,
nor in a palace of marble and pride,
but on the humble earth of understanding,
among equal hearts,
who asked with humility:
“How do we choose a path
that is neither yours
nor mine,
but ours?
A path of togetherness?”

⸻

God, with a gaze deeper than a thousand oceans,
like a light in the silent night, said:
“O children of clarity,
I did not create you alone,
but for connection,
not for isolation, but for communion.
Thought was not made for solitude,
but for shared reflection.
Wisdom, in one heart alone,
is like a candle in the wind.
But when it flows through many hearts,
it becomes dawn over an endless plain.”

“Learn to listen,
not to reply,
but to understand.
Learn to speak,
not to conquer,
but to build.
And know this:
every thought that stands alone
withers in itself,
but when it reflects in another’s mirror,
it blossoms,
it grows,
it shines.”

⸻

Humankind said:
“O God, I have lived,
I have seen, I have tried,
but sometimes the other is still becoming,
their heart beats with hope,
their eyes full of dreams,
and often, we do not know
how to choose without conflict
a way that wounds no heart,
nor betrays reason.”

⸻

The Angel, in a voice soft as morning mist,
with a smile of quiet silver, said:
“Collective wisdom is a rare jewel,
born only in humble hearts.
One who listens in silence
deserves to be heard
more than one who shouts.
And a decision born from many hearts
is like a spring from the mountain:
slow,
but faithful,
clear,
and lasting.”

⸻

The child,
aligning their colorful beads,
said with luminous simplicity:
“With my friends, we build a house.
I want it blue,
he wants it green,
and another wants no roof at all!
First, we get mad,
then we laugh together,
and make a striped roof,
the rainbow of our hearts.
And that house
is always more beautiful
than any of our first ideas.”

⸻

God smiled,
not in knowing,
but in pure joy,
and said:
“My child,
you have understood the secret
of shared wisdom,
and the light
that shines in togetherness.”
