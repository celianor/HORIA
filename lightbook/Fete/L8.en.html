Collective Wisdom, Light of Unity

In the warmth of life’s enchanting table,
Where melodies of love dance in the breeze,
And smiles bloom like flowers in hearts,
Where eyes gaze at one another with longing,
Words are not tools for superiority, but bridges of closeness.
This time, the talk was about collective wisdom,
And the virtue of decisions born from all opinions,
Not from the power of one alone.

— — —

They gathered,
Not on a high podium,
Nor in a palace of proud marble,
But on the simple ground of understanding,
Among equal hearts,
Asking humbly:
“How do we choose a path,
Not mine,
Nor yours,
But ours,
The path of union?”

— — —

God said, with a gaze deeper than a thousand oceans,
Like light in the silent night:
“O children of light,
I did not create you to be alone,
But to be together,
Not in the unity of solitude,
But in the bond of union.
Thought is not for isolation,
But for consultation.
Wisdom in one heart
Is like wax in the wind.
But when it flows in many hearts,
It is dawn over endless plains.”

“Learn to listen,
Not to reply,
But to understand.
Learn to open tongues,
Not to dominate,
But to build.
And know:
Every idea left alone,
Withers in itself,
But if it sees itself in another’s mirror,
It blossoms,
Grows,
And sparkles.”

— — —

Man said:
“O Lord, I have tried,
And seen and tested for years,
But sometimes the other is new.
His heart beats,
His eyes full of dreams,
And sometimes, we do not know
How to choose a path without conflict,
That does not hurt the heart,
Nor disdain the mind.”

— — —

The angel said in a voice soft as morning dew,
And a smile silver-colored like peace:
“Collective wisdom is a rare jewel,
That grows only in humble hearts.
He who listens silently
Is worthy to be heard,
Not the one who shouts.
And decisions born from the heart of the group,
Are like springs from the mountain’s heart,
Calm,
Yet steady,
Pure,
And sincere.”

— — —

In that moment, a child said,
While arranging the beads of his colorful toy,
With radiant simplicity:
“My friends and I build a house.
I want the roof blue,
He wants it green,
The third doesn’t want a roof at all!
At first, we are sad,
But then we laugh together,
And build a striped roof,
A rainbow for our hearts.
And always, that house
Is more beautiful than anyone’s choice alone.”

— — —

God smiled,
Not from knowledge,
But from joy in such simplicity,
And said:
“O my child,
You have known the secret of unity.
Beauty is not in the dominance of one opinion,
But in the blending of colors,
And in union.
What emerges from the hearts of the group,
Is greater than the imagination of any individual,
And is the sacred path born from ‘we.’”

— — —

Man said, with clearer eyes,
And a calmer heart:
“So, O Lord,
For knowledge,
We must learn to listen.
For decision,
We must equalize hearts,
And for building,
We must overcome the pride of a single mind,
And turn to the embrace of collective wisdom.”

— — —

The angel said:
“Yes,
Where many good hearts flow,
And many awake minds,
And many sincere tongues,
Like rivers pouring into one sea,
Wisdom becomes light,
And decisions
Gain the color of God,
Not in one mind,
But in the harmony of the group.”

— — —

And the table of life lit anew.
Not from one light,
But from a thousand candles,
Lit together.
And the sky carried a silent love:
“A good fate,
Springs from hearts beating together,
Not from one hand
Trembling in the wind.”
