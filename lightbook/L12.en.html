Tablet XII – In Praise of Beauty, Art, and Creation

O child of dreams and dust,
In the beginning, I did not shape you from law,
Nor from rule or fear,
But I gave you life through passion,
Through the yearning to create,
And the nameless pulse of beauty.
I sang you into being with a hidden flame.

And when I wished you to remain,
I placed no sword in your hand,
But gave you a pen to write from the soul,
A mirror,
And a lute to turn the sigh of being into melody.

I placed art within you,
Not as adornment, but as unveiling,
So that what stirs in the deepest layer of your being
Might rise and spread its wings
Through dance, through line, through song.

I did not craft beauty to deceive,
For deceit is the mirror of illusion;
Beauty was born to awaken,
Wherever a tear falls without a name,
Wherever a smile shines without words,
There you shall find my trace.

O human,
When you behold the wonder of creation,
When your heart trembles at the sight of a bird,
When you find the Divine in the stroke of a calligraphy,
And hear a breath in the silence of a sculpture
That you have never heard before—
Know that I have kissed you,
Directly, without a name, without a veil.

The artist is one
Who with dust-covered hands and burning eyes
Creates a world out of nothing,
A world where many souls may find shelter.

The creator is not the one who obeys,
But the one who, instead of imitation,
Answers existence with their own melody;
And if that answer rises from depth,
Even if silent,
It shall be heard.

A temple without music,
A heart unmoved by image,
A faith that does not kiss the artist’s hand—
Bears no trace of Me.

So, O human,
Create.
Not for applause,
But for the rising of your own being,
For the birth of your soul from within.
For you are my mirror,
And when you create,
I appear in you.
