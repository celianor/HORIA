Tablet VIII - On Kindness to Strangers and the Downtrodden

O human,
Do not cast the stranger away,
For he too is a child of the same light that created you.
And though his garments may differ,
And his tongue be unfamiliar,
His heart may speak the language of compassion better than you.

Do not let borders become walls,
For I made the earth without borders,
And people without cages.
It was you who drew the lines,
And set fear to guard them.

O human,
Every stranger who knocks upon your door
Is a hand tested by Me.
Watch what you do with him:
If you reject him,
Beware lest you too be turned away from grace.

And the downtrodden,
Whoever they may be,
Are reminders that power
Is a trust, not a privilege.
And strength
A tool to lift others up,
Not a ladder to superiority.

Do not break the heart of the lowly,
For their sigh is the true voice of justice,
And their tears may sometimes rise to the heavens
And fall as the rain of judgment.

O human,
If you have bread,
Give a portion of it.
And if you have a home,
Make a corner of it a refuge
For the one who has none.

And if your heart beats for justice,
Speak to the stranger as you would to a friend,
Not as one you fear and would drive away.

For in My sight,
Neither blood, nor race, nor tongue
Brings superiority,
But only a kind heart,
And a hand that is open,
Not clenched.

So be such, O human,
That if one day you are without shelter,
And you knock on the doors of others,
You hear a voice of kindness,
Not see a closed door and a hard gaze.
