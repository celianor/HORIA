Tablet X – On Freedom of Thought and Faith

O child of earth and stars,
I placed thought within your being,
Not as shackles upon your feet,
But as wings upon your shoulders,
That you may rise from the dust,
And soar beyond the horizons of meaning.

I granted you flight,
And I did not will chains for you.
I sowed longing in your heart,
And deemed fear unworthy.

If you fear to think,
And if you obey blindly without fear,
Then you have strayed far from Me,
Even if you utter My name night and day.

Do not entrust faith to the hands of coercion,
Nor pour it into the chalice of fear,
For that which is born of compulsion
Is like mist upon a mirror—
It brings neither light nor endurance.

I am with the one
Who dares to ask and will not be silent,
Who pauses at the gate of certainty,
Who sees doubt not as a thorn,
But as a seed of truth,
And in the end,
Chooses Me with a free heart.
For only such choosing
Is filled with My light.

O human,
Let no one think in your place,
And do not imprison yourself
In a creed without understanding,
In a book devoid of spirit,
Or in lifeless imitation.

Whoever forbids your questioning,
And turns faith into a spear for your eye,
Is not of Me,
Though My name may adorn their banner.

And the one who thinks freely,
And seeks goodness with sincerity,
Though they walk another path,
If they come with a heart turned to love,
They are welcome at My table.

I, Horia,
Shine in the fire of reason,
Not in the smoke of superstition.
And in the free melody of faith,
Not in the blind cries of fanaticism.

So, O human,
If you seek to know Me,
First, come to know yourself.
And if you would speak of Me,
Let your words arise
From a heart unbound and a fearless tongue.
