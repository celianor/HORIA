Tablet IV – On Freedom of Thought and Expression

O human,
I have granted you thought,
not as a burden on your shoulders,
but as wings for your soul,
so you may descend into your depths,
and from there,
soar to the horizons of knowledge.

Thought, when imprisoned,
is like a bird
that gazes not at the sky,
but at the ceiling of its cage.
And speech, when it rises in fear,
is a half-life yearning to break free;
it neither grows nor enlightens.

O human,
Give every thought a chance to speak,
even the one that tastes bitter,
for truth is sometimes hidden
in a cup of bitterness.
And listen to every voice,
for the ear is noble
when it hears even the harshest tones
without flinching,
or losing sight of the truth.

I do not ask you to believe every thought,
but to release each one
into the field of dialogue,
so your soul
may choose its own path.
For in silencing others,
there is no victory,
no awakening,
but only the withering of the spirit
and the extinction of the soul’s light.

O human,
the one who fears another’s speech
may be a herald of a truth
they cannot bear to hear.
And the one who seals mouths
with the sickle of fear
is neither a guardian of faith
nor a protector of knowledge;
he is a shadow
cast upon his own soul
more than an enemy of others.

Let no word dry
upon another’s lips,
and let no thought
perish in imposed silence.
For when mouths are sealed,
hearts too grow quiet,
and from dead hearts,
no beautiful melody
can arise.
