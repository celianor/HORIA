Tablet Six – On Protecting the Earth and Animals

O human,
I did not spread the Earth for your dominance,
nor to wear a crown and rule over it.
I created you to be its companion—
like a gardener with a luminous heart,
who finds pride not in soil, but in union with it.
Look at the shrub sprouting through stone,
or the bird praying with the breeze.
The Earth speaks to you—
not with the tongue of people,
but with the trembling of leaves,
the bubbling of springs,
the rising of mist,
and the silence of dewdrops on wheat fields.
Take from her, yes,
for she is generous,
but take in measure of your worth,
not the measure of your greed.
For every grain unjustly taken
will return as pain—
in your soul,
in your child,
in the air you breathe.
O human,
I created animals too—
not as ornaments in your markets,
but as silent melodies of creation’s song.
Do not call them your subjects;
call them your kin.
Read from their eyes;
for the cry of a forsaken fawn,
or the distant song of a great whale,
are words from the book of silent existence.
Do not strike the root of a tree
whose shade you have not yet tasted.
Do not slay a tree for the beauty of your home—
for a home without air is but a polished tomb.
Do not let rivers be silenced,
or skies be robbed of their stars,
so the ceilings of cities remain no lower
than the ceilings of your dreams.
The Earth
is not a mute servant of obedience,
but your noble, patient mother.
And a mother is not to be drained until dry,
nor burned for a fleeting light.
O human,
if you let wisdom guide your hands,
and sow compassion into your gaze,
the Earth will bloom again,
and animals, unafraid,
will walk beside you,
and your child, tearless,
will run through meadows.
And know this:
As long as seeds of kindness are cast into hearts,
and hands remain to plant a sapling,
hope will rise from the soil,
and the Earth will smile.
