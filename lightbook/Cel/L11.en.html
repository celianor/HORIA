Tablet XI – In the Mirror of Wisdom and Divine Decree

O human,
No command from Me was engraved in stone,
no order descended in lightning from the skies;
rather, I wrote the just path
in the whispers of hearts,
in the pain of shared sorrow,
and in the longing that dreams of liberation.

When you gather together,
and gaze into truth with open eyes,
and let love weigh your judgments,
and see your own good in the good of all,
there I am—
like a gentle breeze,
like a silent light,
among you.

A decree that falls from the heavens
without looking to the earth
is no message of revelation,
but a stone
cast upon hearts—
not a rain that revives the soul's stream.

O human,
Ask not of Me a decree eternal,
untouched by test or change;
for all that lives
grows with time,
and all that stays unmoved
lies in its own grave.

The decree of yesterday,
if not lit by the light of today,
becomes shadow.
What was written for one people
may not fit another—
like a garment too tight.

So open your eyes,
and open your heart,
and place the heavenly command
in the hands of gardeners,
not as chains on the feet of passers-by.

Where the law
gives no shelter to the weak,
and the roof of justice
shelters not all people,
that command is not from Me.
And where women are counted in halves,
and children are silenced,
and the faithless are rightless,
there,
My light does not shine.

The divine decree,
if not mingled with wisdom,
if not burning with compassion,
if not tempered by experience,
is but a loud robe,
empty of true life.

O humanity,
gather together,
with listening ears,
and hearts without bitterness,
and write a law
that reflects the whole,
not the will of one.

And in that law,
let there be flowing hope, not fear;
let love rise, not wrath.

I, Hora,
honor that command
which you raise
with clear hands
and free hearts
upon the earth of kindness.
