Tablet XVI – On Friendship, Love, and Human Bonds

O human being,
I did not create you to be alone;
for solitude is a dwelling of fear,
and love is revealed only in the mirror of another.

I created you in pairs,
not only in the language of the body,
but in the language of the heart.
For the heart, without the other,
is like a seed in the desert,
without root and without growth.

Every friendship is a bridge
between your soul and my light.
And every love
is a spark of my eternal sun,
kindling two lives.

Where the gaze grows gentle,
where a hand reaches out without demand,
where the presence of another
lightens the burden of life,
there I dwell.

And those who taint friendship with trade,
who weigh love on the scales,
who build bonds upon possession,
have strayed far from me,
even if my name is upon their lips.

O human being,
know that friendship is a gift,
not a transaction.
And love is the art of seeing the other,
not of chaining them.

Do not break another’s heart;
for perhaps that heart was my sanctuary.
Do not enslave another’s body;
for perhaps that body was the mirror
where my light was reflected.

Where two hearts,
unchained and unpretending,
seek each other in tenderness,
there paradise begins.

And the children born of such a bond
are like rain
that quenches the thirsty earth of the world.

Bond is responsibility,
not ownership.
Friendship is an exercise in listening,
not a cry of dominance.

And love,
is remembrance of me,
clothed in the garment of the human.
