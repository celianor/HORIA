Tablet Fifteen — On Freedom, Choice, and Individual Responsibility

Home Back

I did not create you to bind you, nor to place upon you a collar of blind obedience, nor to chain you in imitation. I made you from yourself, that you might be yourself.

Freedom is not the blood-stained heritage of warriors, nor the gift of rulers. Freedom is what I set within your core, like a lamp which, even if its shade is drawn, still continues to shine.

You have choice— to triumph or to fail, to build or to burn. But never will you be free from answering for what you have done.

Choice is no child's play, nor an excuse for wrath. Choice is a hidden covenant between you and me, that whatever you speak, and whatever you seek, returns from you to you.

O human, if you choose ignorance, you darken the world. And if you choose truth, the world will glow by your blessing.

I do not drive you away, nor drag you into bondage; I open the paths before you, and it is you who make the choosing.

So forgive yourself, if once you took a path that was dark. But if you knew that path and stayed within it again, be strict with yourself.

O human, I created freedom for the knowing of truth. And truth, once known, brings responsibility.

Whoever is free bears the world's weight upon their shoulders. And whoever knows is like a lantern held by a caravan through the night.

Accept, then: that your choices shape your world. And your deeds today become the fate of many tomorrows.

You are not only for yourself; you are answerable also to those who have yet to come.
