Tablet V – On Education and the Child

O human,
When a child is born,
it is a part of your being,
a spark from the fire of My essence.
It is the echo of a future yet to come,
a gentle light
shining from your lantern of hope upon the earth.

Be with them
as one is with a sprout in the soil;
not with the flood of commands,
nor with the blade of force,
but with the dew of kindness,
the sun of compassion,
and the patient whisper of presence.

Education, if mixed with fear,
does not bear knowledge,
but becomes a cold shadow
upon the child’s soul,
from which they will flee,
not knowing why.

Do not teach them
to become like you;
teach them
so they may find themselves,
and create a new path—
one your own imagination has not yet reached.

Do not make their memory a stone tablet;
make their heart a field of rain,
for if their heart is free,
knowledge will blossom within,
like a joyful flower in the spring rain.

O human,
every word you utter
is either a bridge to the world,
or a wall upon the soul.
And every glance of yours
is either a wing to soar,
or a chain of fear.

Therefore, learn silence before speaking,
and humility before teaching;
for the true teacher
teaches through their presence,
not through words alone.

Give them the lesson of life,
not through command,
nor through prohibition,
but through your being:
if you seek love, be loving;
if you seek truth, be true.

I have placed
the silent secrets of the universe in the soul of the child;
do not let them be extinguished
in the noise of rules and reproach,
so they may not forget
who they are,
and where they came from.

The future you hope for
will grow from their small hands,
not from your words or wishes.
So today,
be with them in such a way
that tomorrow,
they will remember you—
not with a heart full of anger,
but with a soft smile,
like the scent of a bright memory.
