Tablet Fourteen

On Peace, Patience, and Coexistence
From the Book of Hidden Light

O human,
I did not fashion you from dust
So you would boast of your own soil
Or lower a veil of hatred
Over a handful of earth.
I summoned you from light,
So you may shine, like a lamp in the wind,
Not only for yourself,
But for the passing stranger.
For if you are light,
And the other, too, is light,
Then night shall find no strength against you.

I did not create you
To draw lines between hearts
Or build walls between souls
In My name.
My name is not a sword,
But a shield and sanctuary;
Not a border or barricade,
But a bridge between hearts.

Whoever deems themselves a singular jewel
And sees others as stone or dust—
Though they praise My name a thousand times,
Their heart holds no share of My light.
For My breath does not fall
On those who see none but themselves upon the summit.

But one who sits at the table of the stranger,
Eats the bread of humility,
And hears a foreign song
Without thorns in the ear of the heart—
That one knows My soul,
Without opening any book of faith
Or bearing a mark upon the brow.

Whoever looks with kindness,
Be they with religion or without,
Speaking your tongue or silent,
Wearing your color or waving another banner—
If their heart is made of light
And their shadow harms no one,
They are kin to the radiance,
And in this caravan of creation,
They are your housemate of the soul.

If anyone sheds blood in My name,
I will turn My face away.
For My name is a cup of love,
Not a poison for strife.

And if the memory of Me
Sparks a fire in someone’s heart
Not of longing, but of hatred,
That memory shall fade from My mind.
For My flame warms the soul—
It does not burn it down.

Peace is not merely the silence of swords,
But the gentle song of birds
On the branch of a tree
Whose roots reach into understanding,
And whose shade stretches over patience.

Patience
Is not silence born of fear,
But the dignity of wisdom.
Know that each person
Is a traveler upon a path—
Many paths, perhaps,
But the destination is one.

The one who shouts in the name of truth
Is not necessarily closer,
But the one who polishes their own mirror
And gazes inward
Before pointing out the flaws of others,
That one draws nearer to My threshold.

Whoever makes of faith not a fortress,
But a window toward compassion,
And sees love
As wider than tongue, race, or nation—
I will behold them circling the light.

O human,
From whichever road you come—
If your hand has soothed another’s wound,
And your gaze recites a poem of kindness,
I shall embrace you,
Not for who you are,
But for the light you carry.

The world is not the home of a single tribe,
And I am not the god of one tongue or chosen clan.
I dwell in hearts
That, like springs,
Flow for all,
And deny water to none.

I am a shelter above radiant hearts,
A refuge for those
Who believe in love—
Not in borders.
