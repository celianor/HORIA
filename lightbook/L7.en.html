Tablet VII – On Truth and the Oversight of Power

O human,
I did not create power for you to rule,
but to serve,
not upon a throne, but among the people,
like a hand that holds a lantern,
not one that burns the possessions of others.
Where power is left unchecked,
temptation lies in wait,
and truth is the first victim of thrones unchallenged.
Whoever gains power
bears a burden of trust,
not a goblet of pride.
And whoever blinds himself to his own faults,
and deafens his ears to the people,
even if he speaks in the name of justice,
is far from My path.
O human,
truth is the canopy of power.
Without truth,
power turns into oppression,
like stagnant water that becomes foul.
So whoever you entrust with a task,
test their truthfulness, not their voice.
And whoever you raise higher,
demand greater accountability,
not greater freedom.
And when you yourself hold power,
seek a mirror,
not blind flatterers.
For you too are made of dust,
and error dwells within your nature.
Therefore, establish laws,
not to dominate,
but to protect yourself from yourself,
so that when you forget,
they may remind you.
O human,
all power without the people's eye is blind,
and without free questioning, it goes mad and tyrannical.
Let the watchers question,
and take no offense,
for that is guardianship, not ingratitude.
I cherish the ruler
who obeys the people.
And I despise the one who, in My name,
sews mouths shut,
and chains the truth.
