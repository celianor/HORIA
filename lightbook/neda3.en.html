The Third Call: Your Now, Your Mirror, the Song of Hura

O child of long times and bright paths,
You are now in the middle,
Between who you were and who you will become.
You are neither a silent child nor a creator of light,
Yet you carry the sign of both within yourself.

This world, where you walk on its soil and breathe its air,
Is neither your exile nor a place for idleness,
But a school for your soul,
And a mirror that reflects you to yourself.

When I sent you to this abode,
I planted a lamp in your heart called “Conscience,”
Which, without books or language,
Recognizes the path from the wrong,
And friend from foe.

This conscience is like a stream,
That if you don’t throw stones in it,
Will have clear water.
And if you dig it,
In its depths, you will see my reflection.

O you who are engaged in living,
Do not think life is a game without a judge.
The judge sits within you,
And its melody is the voice of your true self.

You are now in the time called life,
And life is another name for opportunity.

The opportunity to see, to know,
To do good,
And to create a better self.

O dear possibility,
I have granted you this time,
Which is like a garden in four seasons:
In its spring, wishes bloom,
In its summer, experiences ripen,
In its autumn, judgments fall,
And in its winter, the silence of contemplation
Prepares you for another spring.

So do not waste life,
For every moment is a precious pearl
That once lost cannot be found again.

Have you not seen the river?
If it does not flow, it does not stay clear.
Your life is the same;
In stagnation, it becomes a swamp,
But in flow, a sea.

O beloved one,
Who came from my love,
Do not say life is hard,
For hardship is the test of your strength.
Just as the sword is tempered in fire,
The human heart is polished through suffering.

Do good, not out of fear of reward,
But out of the joy of light.
Be kind,
For kindness is a seed that grows in your own field.
And when your long night comes,
Only your good deeds will be your lantern.

O seeker of light,
Life is not for accumulating wealth,
But for adding to your being.
And being,
Is to grow by blooming from within.

I did not create you to merely survive,
If that were so,
I would have been content with your plant-like existence.
I gave you life,
To give meaning.

Every word that leaves your mouth,
Either builds a bridge or a wall.
Every step you take,
Leads either to light,
Or to shadow.
And I neither take from you,
Nor add to you,
Except what you create with your own hands and heart.

So this life is a stage for you;
I watch,
But the role is in your hands.
And you are not only the actor,
You are also the writer.
With every choice,
You write a new line in the book of creation.
