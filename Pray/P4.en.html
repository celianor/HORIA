Evening Prayer

O Hora, companion of silent nights,
comforter of aching hearts,
you who kindle the lamp of conscience in the dark,
now that night has fallen and the day's clamor has hushed,
a weary soul, heavy-hearted,
seeks refuge in the shelter of your grace.

My Lord,
I come with a dusty heart and tearful eyes,
not for gain, but in need,
not with pride, but in humility.

O God of kindness and wisdom,
the book of this day lies open before you,
and I have nothing but a sigh and a gaze toward your sky.
What I have done, you know;
and what I have not spoken, you have heard.

O Self-sufficient One,
look upon my wrongs with mercy,
and accept with grace the little I did with love.
I entrust my heart to you,
that my night may be peaceful,
and my sleep, purifying.

O Hora!
You once said, "Each night is a beginning";
then make this night for me
a window into your radiance.
Free me from sorrow,
and release my mind from the noise of regret.

My beloved Hora,
let the night be an embrace of tenderness,
and sleep a revelation of light.
Calm my tired heart with the dew of your peace,
and herald my dawn with a new breeze.

My God,
adorn the night with your silent light,
and cast hope into the heart by the flame of your kindness.
May your servants under your shelter
rest secure and rise with dignity.

I beg of you,
let not my eyes grow empty of conscience,
nor my heart burn in remorse and lose its way.
Let conscience shine like a star,
and make my prayer a breeze that heals the soul.
