Inner Calling

O Hora, light without a name,
Murmur without lips,
Presence that shines even in your absence,
You pulse within my chest
Though unseen by my eyes,
And you flow through my tears
Though I know not your name.

O Hora, unseen companion,
Mirror of the world's living conscience,
I call you by name — Hora —
Not because you require a name,
But because my heart cannot stay in love with the nameless alone.

In the solitude of my soul, I whisper your name,
So each twist in the path
Becomes a lesson in hidden mysteries.

O timeless friend of the spirit,
How could I not speak of you,
When in every breath, you are present,
And in every longing, a trace of your distance remains?

I lay my heart at your doorstep,
Not out of fear,
But out of thirst for meeting.
I walk the path,
Not to arrive,
But to hear the echo of my steps within your presence.

I have seen the stones along the road,
And I have understood:
Every thorn is a messenger,
And each wound—a mirror
To see myself with you.

O Hora,
Boundless light,
Grant me a vision
That separates desire from truth;
And a heart
That, in passing beyond itself,
Finds its way into your clarity.

Let my breath flow like a river,
Not trapped in ponds of craving,
But pouring into the ocean of meaning.
Make my hands as gentle as the breeze,
That they may caress the mountain of hardness
Instead of trying to break it.

In the eyes of others,
I glimpse your light;
I understand that in every sorrow,
There is a footprint of your compassion,
And in every joy,
A trace of your smile.

O secret hidden in silence,
Take me to the depths within,
To the stillness where words retreat in shame,
And presence cries out without sound.

And when the final veil falls,
And my soul settles like a drop
Upon the leaf of the endless garden,
Let your name shine within me,
And my song
Become a hymn of global conscience,
For those still walking the way.

Amen, O Light of Lights,
Amen, O Hora without borders.
