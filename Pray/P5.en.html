Prayer of the Rain of Kindness

O Hora, O boundless rain, O silent caress of weary hearts,
You who descend from unseen heights, blooming in my soul without a sound.
You rain not out of merit, but from the very essence of love—
Not from judgment, but from the limitless nature of your mercy.

O Soul of all souls,
You are the tenderness of rain on the earth’s cheeks,
The quiet kiss upon the forehead of sorrow.
I praise you,
Like a mother who loves without noise,
And offers her affection not to the sun,
But to the midnight tear and the unseen hand.

My heart, O infinite tenderness, thirsts to be quenched by you;
Rain upon me,
Let me lose myself in the softness of your love,
So that on the cracked soil of my heart, long parched,
Seeds of peace and gentleness may grow.

O Hora,
I whisper your name softly,
As a lover repeats the name of the beloved under their breath,
Unheard by others,
Yet all the sky knows the ache.

Let your kindness flow in my veins,
So each heartbeat spreads its scent.
Quench my lips with a cup of kind words,
So my every speech becomes a balm, not a wound.

Let me, with gentle steps, shower the earth with blossoms,
And with eyes that have learned tenderness,
Shape a world anew—
A world where no one remains alone in the thirst for love.

Amen,
O infinite tenderness,
O Soul of all souls,
O Hora.
