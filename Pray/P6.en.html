Prayer of Awakening and Perseverance

In the name of the enlightening Soul, the creative Mind, and the love that shines in the chest

O exalted meaning, O hidden song within the soul, O radiance that comes neither from sun nor star, But shines from the awakened self, I call you, not from fear nor greed, But from longing to bloom, from the passion of dignity! You who have no name, yet I have given you a thousand names, No name suits you, except the one truly lived! I bring myself before myself, Until reason within me collapses, And a new greatness rises from old ashes. I seek no refuge, for you did not create us to hide, But to rise, to kindle, to create, to fight if needed, And to love, if we can. Teach me to be a light, not a shadow; To be a tongue, not silence; To be wisdom, not humiliation. Let not the empty rosary replace reflection, Let not fear overpower hope, Let not lies, clothed in faith, deceive hearts. O inner Horia, O majesty of freedom, Fill me with a heart full of voice, So that if silence comes, it is conscious; And if a cry comes, it is in its place. Free us from servitude, even that of the sky, For you wished us to be human, not fallen; Creators of ourselves, not enslaved creatures. And to whoever is stripped of love, light, truth, Give strength to return, Not by sword, but by true presence of self. Greetings to those who spread light without force, And kindle fire only to warm, And create gods not for worship, But to remind of the highest stature of man. Hōram, Hōram, Hōram! O unfinished word, O opened path!
