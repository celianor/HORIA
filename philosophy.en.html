Horia is a religion as old as human history.

Not a religion for obedience to religious rulers or oppressors, but one for awakening, peaceful coexistence, and devotion to collective wisdom — while protecting individual freedoms.

Its prophets are many — thousands, named and unnamed, among the people.

Its temples are everywhere people gather to decide with kindness: in schools, councils, communities.

Horia is the continuation of Socrates’ quest, the echo of Zoroaster and Kant, the vision of Spinoza, and the dream of kind, life-loving humans.

In Horia, no one speaks for God — yet every human can hear the divine.

This religion was not made for domination, but for liberation;

Not for a fake heaven in the sky, but for building a real one on Earth.
