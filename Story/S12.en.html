In the Mirror of Will and Wisdom

I asked: “Who are you?”

He said: “The one others expect me to be.”

I replied: “Then you are neither yourself nor your essence; a mirror that reflects only the world outside.”

Another said: “I follow my own path.”

But he was faulted for worshipping isolation as if it were truth.

Will, if it rises from the soul but turns away from the world, becomes either passion without guidance or path without fire.

Unbridled will turns to peril,
but chained by reason, it grows inert

A tree without roots does not grow, and roots without light do not thrive.
Wisdom is the root, will is the light.
One without the other bears neither branch nor fruit.

To know oneself is the start of the path;
to understand with others is its perfection.

A youth set out on a journey and said: “Why seek advice when I already know all?”

He carried a map in his mind, but did not know the signs of the road.

He wandered lost for three days, until he reached a stream.

An old man sat there and said: “Had you asked, you would already have arrived.”

The youth replied: “I did not want to be indebted to anyone.”

The elder smiled: “Debt belongs to the idle, not the seekers. To ask is to be awake.”

Listen to the wise, and set sail—
for it is with the help of the crew that the captain charts the waves

Collective wisdom without individual will falls asleep in silence;
and the lone will, without seeking common insight, burns itself out.

Unjust will becomes tyranny;
blind obedience becomes servitude.

Ask, even if you think you know the answer—
for the question is a window to the light,
and only in light can one tell the path from the false trail.

If wisdom is not weighed on the scale of the many,
how can right be told from wrong?
And one who shatters the mirror—
how will he see the measure of his beauty or his flaw?

Will is command, and wisdom is compass.
To rely on one without the other is either stagnation or misdirection.

One has no lamp, and sees nothing at night;
another holds a lamp, but is lost in the desert

He who rises by his own will becomes as firm as the mountain;
he who seeks counsel from the just flows like a river;
and one who unites both becomes a tree—rooted in earth, reaching the sky.

Do not mistake will for stubbornness;
nor counsel for submission.
Counsel is an invitation to clarity, not a debt;
and will is persistence in truth, not insistence on self.

A bird was born in a cage; it knew nothing of flight.
Though the door was open, it believed the cage was home.

Another had wings but knew not the wind; its flight was short and weary.

A third had wings and knew the art of soaring;
when the wind rose, it rose and flew.

Reach yourself, and you do not forsake the world;
Reach the world, and you do not lose yourself
