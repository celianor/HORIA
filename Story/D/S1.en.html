The Day Kindness Took Wing

Dara heard a sound.
Not a human voice, nor the whisper of wind,
but a trembling chirp, from beyond a silent hedge.

He stopped. He listened.
And his heart, without knowing why, began to beat differently.
A fragile sound of life—wounded, helpless—
among thorns, dust, and fear.

Dian gently said:
"Come look… it’s a chick…
So tiny… but it’s hurt. Its wing is torn, like an unfinished dream."

The chick, smaller than a dry twig,
had curled into itself;
its eyes, like two drops of fear,
gazed at the world
with no shelter but the beat of a small heart.

Dara softly said:
"We have to help it…
but with a calm heart,
not with hurried hands,
nor with a shadow of fear."

Dian slowly reached out her hand,
not like a ruler claiming a thing,
but like a mother laying her hand upon kindness.

The chick trembled,
but found refuge.
In that moment, something happened:
silent trust,
between a tiny bird and two children,
passed like light between mirrors.

Dian said:
"It’s so small… but its heart…
it beats… just like ours."

Dara smiled.
Not a smile of knowledge,
but of understanding.
"No one is too small to matter…
Perhaps true greatness
is the size of your heart when it beats for someone else."

They took the chick home.
Mother, kind and silent,
washed the wound,
and in a quiet corner of the house,
they made a space softer than clouds,
silent, yet full of hope.

Days passed.
The wound healed,
and the wing remembered again
that it could fly.

That day, the chick flew.
Not with a cry, but with a silent song,
and in the heart of the sky,
it became a dot—
small, but bright.

Dian looked up and said:
"I’m going to miss it…
we won’t see it again…"

Dara said:
"Maybe not with our eyes,
but with our hearts, yes.
Now the sky holds one more healed bird…
and our hearts,
one brighter memory…
of the day kindness took wing."
