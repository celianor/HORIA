Chapter VII: The Rite of Water

In Praise of Freedom, Clarity, and the Voice of Conscience

One day,
Ilara, the lady of luminous soul,
was seated by a spring,
and her companions,
silent and humble,
listened to the murmur of the water.

Then,
like a breeze rising from the mountains,
she began to speak,
and her words
fell like dew upon the leaves of souls.

“If a rite is to be worthy,
let it be like water:
clear,
without violence,
without pretension,
without borders or pride.”

“Water does not command,
yet it opens the way.
Water does not say ‘Be’,
yet wherever it flows,
life springs from it.”

“Let not what is called a rite
become a chain,
for a true rite
is a bond;
not a prison,
but a spring of kindness.”

“Faith
is not a wall that divides,
but a river that nourishes.
If you imprison it, it stagnates;
if you release it,
it nurtures and is nurtured.”

“Keep your belief in your heart,
not in a wall,
not in a fortress,
for the fortress
sends the soul into exile.”

At that moment,
one of the companions asked:

— “Our Lady, is the rite of water without command?”

And Ilara,
smiled a smile that the sun echoed,
and answered:

“No, it is not without command;
but its command
is not a shout of rule from above,
it is the whisper of conscience from within.”

“The rite of water
is a command of love,
not of fear;
a call to light,
not an imposition of shadows.”

“Be such that,
when you pass,
a river remains of you,
not wounds of footsteps,
nor the shadow of fear.”

“Live in such a way
that they do not ask who you are,
but say of you:
‘In their presence, there is a light
that does not go out.’”

“The rite of water
is the rite of flowing and giving,
not of harshness,
not of staged submission.”

“Its prayer
is to laugh with the soul of others,
and its offering
is compassion for the pains
that are not yours,
but beat within you.”

“If water is our rite,
then our home
is the river;
our garment,
the gentleness of breeze;
and our weapon,
the silent and soothing love
that makes the world bloom without noise.”
