Chapter Six: A House of Light

On the sanctity of coexistence and the building of hearts

One day, Ilara, the luminous-hearted lady,
in the hush of a violet dawn,
laid her hand upon the earth,
and the earth, like an unknowing child, trembled at her whisper.

Then she spoke, not from the throat,
but from the depths of her soul:

“A house,
is where the soul may sit unafraid and rest without fear.
And this refuge,
is not of stone and wall,
but of kindness and truth,
of the light of trust,
and the breeze of shared words.”

And to her companions on the path, she said:

“O you who yearn for a peaceful world,
if you wish the world to be a home,
first,
adorn your hearts with gentleness,
so every passerby may find shelter within,
and every wound may find its healing there.”

And one, whose voice carried the rustle of doubt, asked:
— “O our Lady, how can we be together?
In a land of many walls,
and so few ears?”

Ilara smiled,
a smile that, like the moon on water, lit up every heart.
And she said:

“To be together,
is not in the embrace, but in the ear;
coexistence is the art of listening,
not merely speaking.
And gentleness,
is the first brick of a house
where every homeless soul
finds refuge.”

Then she spoke of a city
that lives in dreams,
but is near to every awakened heart:

“There is a city,
where people do not strive to win,
but to be with one another.
In that city, the child does not fear punishment,
and the elder does not grieve in forgetfulness.
There,
kindness is not a rule,
but the air
they breathe.”

And she added,
as if inviting one to a sacred secret:

“That house,
stands on three pillars:

First, listening;
not as the ear,
but as the heart,
that weeps at the tremble of truth.

Second, fairness;
not the cold justice of judgment,
but the weighing of a mother
among the children of her soul.

And third,
forgiveness;
not from weakness,
but from a summit
that the soul has reached.”

On a night when the moon was veiled in cloud,
and the stars whispered their lullaby to the sky,
Ilara said:

“Never have I dreamed of a house of stone;
my dream
is a house
where no secret remains hidden:
not sorrow, nor joy,
not question, nor longing.
A house whose roof is made of honesty,
and whose windows open to freedom.”

And like a gentle breeze,
with eyes where a calm sea roared, she added:

“Such a house,
is not built by kings,
nor brought down by messengers of heaven;
but rather,
it is built by people,
with their own hands
and their awakened hearts,
when the heart itself becomes a house of light.”
