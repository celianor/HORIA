Chapter IX: Breaking, and Rising Again

Ilara said:

"No one stands forever,
and no fall is the end of the path."

And on a day when many hearts were heavy,
in a soft voice, like a breeze over ruins, she said:

"If you collapse,
know that your old home had become too small.
If you break,
know that something new within you wished to be born."

To those who were ashamed of their suffering,
Ilara rose and said:

"There is no shame in tears,
no disgrace in falling.
Disgrace lies where your soul is burning,
and you pretend there is no ash."

And then she added:

"I too have been broken,
not once,
but many times.
And each breaking
became a window to the light;
not as a reward,
but as a chance to see
what joy had once hidden from my eyes."

She spoke of suffering:

"Suffering is like water:
it either grows the flower,
or erodes the stone.
And it is you who must choose to be the flower or the stone."

Someone asked:

— "My Lady, what if we fall again and again,
and cannot rise anymore?"

And Ilara replied:

"Rising is not always of the body;
sometimes, simply having hope
is a form of rising.
Sometimes, merely staying alive
is proof that the light has not gone out."

She also said:

"The more broken one is,
the more light shines from within;
for it is through the cracks
that the light finds its way."

And in the quiet night that closed this chapter,
she sat on the ground and whispered:

"O you who are broken,
know that within you
there is something unbroken—
something even breaking could not destroy.
And that is the light of Hora in you."
