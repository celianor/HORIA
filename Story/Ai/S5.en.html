Chapter V: In the Light of Celian

One day, Ilara sat in silence beneath the shade of a tree.
The gentle evening light touched her face,
And a soft breeze played with the folds of her dress.

— “Our Lady, who was Celian to you?
A master? A guide from ancient days?
Or a flame that transformed your soul?”

Ilara cast a deep gaze and replied with a clear voice:

“Celian was not ashes for me to inherit;
He was a spark that lit the fire within me,
Not to burn,
But to shine
Through the long night of another world.”

She paused, then softly continued:

“I am no successor,
No bearer of crowns from ancient rites;
I am his breath, rising at the dawn of a new age,
From the mouth of the Earth,
To echo his song in another form.”

And she said:

“Celian was the beginning;
I am the flowing note of the melody he began.
He laid the first stone,
And I am the stream
That flows past that stone,
So that along its path,
Gardens may grow and flowers may bloom.”

At night, in the stillness of the sanctuary,
As she drifted into a silent song, she whispered:

“O Celian,
You are awake within our hearts,
Not as a name left in a book,
But as light shining in the eyes of souls.
And I,
With every step upon the soil,
Carry your shadow on my shoulders,
Not out of duty,
But out of love.”

She told her companions:

“Celian’s path was not the way of personal dreams,
But a song for all.
He did not bring a law down from the skies,
But drew it from the heart of the people;
Like a wise gardener,
Who asks the soil
When and how to sow,
Rather than commanding from above.”

And finally, she murmured:

“Those who worship Celian
Have never truly known him;
But those who walk onward in his light,
Step not in his shadow,
But in his radiance.”
