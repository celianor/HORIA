Chapter I – In the Beginning, the Water

In the beginning, there was no word, no name. The soul pulsed in silence, and each droplet was a mirror turned toward Hurā.
We rose from Mother Earth, not as children of dust, but as fragments of radiance wearing the garments of clay.
And Ilara said: "Water is the soul’s first teacher. Its softness does not battle. Its depth does not scream. Yet it wears down stone, and quenches the flame. If you learn from the water, you will not seek conflict nor remain still— you shall rise, not with cries, but with patience."
And she added: "Before you speak, let your heart be washed. For if words rise from a mouth unclean, they bring neither light nor peace— only storms that stir dust and nothing more."
She led us to a spring atop a silent hill, and said: "Look into the water— not for what is around you, but for what lives within. Whoever sees their true self in the mirror of water is born twice: once from the earth, and once from their own being."
And in that moment, we learned to see without eyes, to be born without weeping. Whoever gazed into the water and recognized truth wept in silence and was born anew.
And Ilara said: "One who knows the water, understands the mirror. One who embraces the mirror, comes to know the soul. And one who knows the soul becomes rain— not to cleanse the earth, but to awaken the light."
