Chapter IV: At the Threshold of the Human

One day, Ilara was seated among her companions.
Her eyes were calm, her voice flowed like a gentle breeze.
She began to speak and said:
"Hora did not create the human to be a servant and obey.
He shaped them as a mirror,
so that a glimpse of His own being might reflect.
And that gaze was free… free and radiant."
Then her companions asked:
— "Our Lady, why is the human so lost?"
Ilara replied, with quiet gaze:
"Because the human has forgotten they were born upright,
but lived their life bent and bowed.
They have adorned themselves with names not their own,
and searched for their reflection
in the mirrors of others, not in the sanctuary within."
With a soft smile, she added:
"I see the human as a tree:
its roots are in the earth,
but it drinks from the sky.
O human,
you are not the failed god of some creation,
nor a talking beast alone.
You are a bridge
between darkness and light,
between desire and letting go,
between extinction and rebirth."
Then a woman in the circle asked:
— "Ilara, are we born in sin?"
And Ilara replied:
"No,
sin is not a wound you were born with.
Sin is the moment
when you know, yet ignore;
when you can, but do not;
when you could forgive, but withhold."
And she added:
"Look at the children,
before they speak,
their hearts already sense what is right.
Hora placed truth within their soul,
and law grows from the heart itself—
not from distant decrees,
nor from those who claim to speak for the divine."
Ilara did not see the human as small;
she wished them vast.
Not enslaved,
but awake and free to choose.
At the end of that day,
Ilara softly whispered:
"I stand at the threshold of the human,
not as a teacher,
but as a student
who seeks in every soul
the light of Hora."
