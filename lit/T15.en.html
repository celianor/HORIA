On the Wisdom of the People Over the Rule of the Ruler

In a distant land, a man arrived from a troubled realm where the kingdom had fallen into decay. The kings no longer listened, and the people no longer dared to speak.

As he set foot in a city beneath the sun, he saw cheerful faces, a peaceful spirit, vibrant marketplaces, and hearts at ease.

He asked an old man with a clear gaze and a calm soul:
— “O elder, wise and time-worn, who holds the power here? Who commands and who rules?”

The old man replied:
“We have no sultan and no lord, no single will that dictates, no tyrant that binds. Whoever speaks is heard, and whoever hears silences their pride.
We consult one another — calmly, without clamor — and the decision born of collective reason becomes the path we follow.”

He asked: “But if there is no one who reigns, how are you safe from chaos?”

The old man said:
“When a decision is born from many minds, it is not made lightly. And when hearts are bound together, the hand of corruption is cut short.
In the realm of the sun, authority stems not from rank, but from awareness — not from the sword, but from wisdom.”

As it is well said:
A kingdom guided by the mind of one
Shall falter and fail, undone.
The wise seek counsel from every side,
For a lone heart soon loses its guide.
