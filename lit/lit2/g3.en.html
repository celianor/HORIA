Assembly III – On Doubt and Faith, and the Bond of Heart and Reason

One of the listeners, with eyes torn between belief and uncertainty, asked:

“I want to believe,
but there is doubt in my heart.
I fear being deceived,
fear that my hope may be a mirage.
Is doubt a sign of disbelief?
Must faith be blind?
My reason says one thing,
my heart says another—
which should I follow?”

Horia smiled,
not with judgment,
but with comfort for the soul:

Seeker,

Doubt is not the enemy of faith—
it is often its beginning.
Those who feel nothing do not question.
But your heart questions,
so it is alive, it is awake.

In the path of Horia,
faith is not forced upon anyone;
it is born from the dialogue of heart and reason.

We do not trust in faith born of fear,
nor in that which springs from stubbornness.

The heart is a warm home,
but if its windows are left open,
the winds of illusion may chill it.
And reason is a lantern,
but on its own, sometimes the light is faint in the dark.

Horia says:
let your heart and reason speak to one another.

The heart tells you:
life longs for meaning—
friendship, truth, forgiveness, beauty—
these stir you for a reason.

And reason says:
think, question, don’t be blind;
know your path, and don’t fear knowledge.

Faith in Horia
is neither naive acceptance,
nor endless skepticism;
it is informed hope.

You are like a lover who receives a letter,
not yet sure if it is truly from the beloved.
The heart whispers:
“This handwriting is theirs.”
And the mind replies:
“Let us check the address, seek the signs.”

You neither cast the letter aside,
nor surrender blindly;
you walk forward—
guided by love and clarity.

This is the path of Horia.

And in our way:

Faith is enlightened hope.
Not blind-eyed,
nor closed-hearted.
Neither hostile to reason,
nor enslaved by fear.
