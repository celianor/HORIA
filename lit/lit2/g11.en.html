Majlis Eleven: On Goodness and the Paths of Life’s Renewal

O faithful companion,

We have spoken of death and truth,
of freedom, love, and the future.
Now we step into the realm of goodness—
a quiet and profound force,
that turns the world without noise,
and awakens hearts without seeking credit.

Goodness is like rain falling
unasked upon the dry earth of hearts,
reviving the roots of hope
even in the most silent soils.

Goodness is more than action;
it is a state of being,
a quality of presence,
a mirror of the soul’s inner light.

In Horia, goodness is not what others expect,
nor what law commands,
but what flows from the depths of the soul
when the heart remembers the unity of existence.

Goodness is the silent conversation of soul to soul;
a gaze that consoles,
a silence that absorbs pain,
a hand that rises only to help,
and a heart that aches for another’s wound
without ever calling it sacrifice.

And the renewal of life
begins right here:
with the simplest act of kindness,
a drop of a smile,
a shelter without judgment,
a reminder of what it means to be human.

For life does not bloom in thunder,
but in the gentleness of a blossom.
And the world is not rebuilt by force,
but by seeds of goodness
sown in the heart.

We do good,
not to be seen,
but because we cannot not do it;
for goodness is the echo
of our harmony with the cosmos.

Goodness is a path
from self to unity,
from I to we,
from small self to a soul
opened to the infinite whole.

And every act of goodness, however small,
is a spark in the cold night
of the world’s loneliness—
so hope may once more rise
from the ashes.

And this is the eleventh principle of our path:

Goodness
is the beginning of renewal;
not a duty, but a joy;
not a chain, but a flight.
Goodness is a dialogue between the heart and being.
Every kindness is a light in awakened hearts,
a seed in the garden of the future.
