Assembly 16: On the Play of Life and the Sacred Inner Child

One of the companions, who had walked for years through the alleys of the world wearing the heavy armor of seriousness, gently asked:

“O Horia, how should life be lived?
Should every moment be filled with gravity and effort, as though the weight of the world rests on our shoulders?
Or is there wisdom also in laughter and play?”

Horia smiled — light and free, like the breeze among flowering branches — and said:

Life is a play; not a frivolous or careless game, but a sacred one, where every moment is a gateway to light, and every smile echoes the deepest wisdoms.

The child within us is not mere simplicity, but a heart unmasked — fearless and pure.

They see without judgment,
laugh without reason,
and forgive without expecting anything in return.

Within that child lies a mystery, guarded by the truly wise: one who does not forget their inner child, even amid the storm of days, does not merely survive — they flourish.

Know this:
Play is the wordless prayer of the child,
and laughter, their sacred chant.

And this is the "Nin" in our tradition:

Life is a field of sacred play, not a battlefield for pride and conquest.
The child of the soul is not a distant memory, but a messenger of clarity.
Whoever embraces their childhood without shame draws close to the secret of freedom.
