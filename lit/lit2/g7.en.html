Horia,

O questioner,
Truth is not a narrow stream
that flows in only one bed;
it is the sun
to which a thousand windows open.

If you drink from one spring,
and another from a different one,
are you not both quenched?
Does your thirst
crave the name of the spring,
or the clarity of the water?

We, in Horia, do not say:
only our path is right.
We say:
any path that leads one to love,
to light,
and to liberation,
is precious.

But...

Not every road that resembles truth
leads to it.

A faith that teaches violence,
even in the name of God,
stands far from the light.

A creed that belittles women,
frightens children from joy,
condemns the lover as sinful,

though a thousand verses in it may ring true,
it dries the heart;
just as a criminal may have many good traits,
but a soul void of love.

In our path,
truth lives in clarity.
And every belief
that calls the human being
to freedom,
to kindness,
and to growth,
is no stranger‚Äî
whatever its name may be.

We are not here to erase,
nor to rule.
We are here for dialogue,
for the fellowship of hearts,
and to see truth not as possession,
but as radiance.

And this is the seventh lantern in our way:

Truth is no wall;
it is the sun.
There are many paths,
but not all lead to the light.
And that path is true
which awakens the soul
to love and to freedom.
