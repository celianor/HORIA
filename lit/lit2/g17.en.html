Session Seventeen: In the Silence and Depth of Stillness

Someone quietly asked, with a tender voice:

"Why does the heart sometimes stop speaking?
And why is there in silence something that a thousand words cannot hold?"

Horia answered, calmly and without rush,
as if silence itself was a word spoken:

Silence, yes,
is not the failure of speech,
but the opening of a gate to the meaning beyond words.

In a world filled with noise and endless words,
the soul grows weary of constant sound,
and longs
to listen without noise,
to understand without utterance.

Silence is not a cold loneliness,
but a warm and tender refuge;
a presence full of life;
a place where the soul
converses with Being
without opening lips,
without uttering a word.

In Horia,
silence is the soil where meanings sprout;
an open field for hidden mysteries;
and whoever befriends silence
dwells with a pure truth.

How many voices are heard only in silence,
how many melodies, lost in the turmoil of words,
sing like nightingales at dawn
in silent hearts...

And in our tradition, it is thus:

Silence
is another language of presence;
those who listen within themselves
will see the world with new eyes.
Silence is not a void,
but an open embrace of Being,
where the soul finds peace and light.
