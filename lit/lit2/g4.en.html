Assembly IV: On the Mystery of Creation and the Why of Existence

One who looked up to the heavens and listened deep within asked:

“Where did we come from?
Why did this world come to be?
Was everything born of blind chance?
Or was there a design behind it?”

If there is a Creator,
why is He silent?
Why does He not reveal to us the secret of being?”

Horia replied,
not with harsh judgment,
but with gentle thought
and the warmth of longing that embraces even doubt:

O seeker of truth,
you are not alone.
All awakened hearts
have opened their eyes with this same first question.

In Horia,
no one says:
“God created the world in six days,”
nor says:
“No hand was present in the making of the universe.”

In the words of Horia:
The world is the song of God.
Not whispered secretly to self-proclaimed prophets,
but echoed in every tree and star,
every atom and every heart.

The universe is not a blind accident;
there is order in it,
beauty,
growth and transformation,
a movement from simplicity to awareness,
from rawness to understanding,
from namelessness to the desire for meaning.

If you ask:
“Why did He not create us complete and without need? Why so flawed and fragile?”
Horia whispers:
Because it is in the becoming that greatness lies.
If the beginning were already the end,
what joy would there be in climbing?
Where would be the delight of finding after the search?
What would hope for knowledge mean, if all were already known?

In Horia’s view,
God painted a living canvas—beautiful, yet dynamic and seeking perfection,
like a loving gardener,
who planted a seed of goodness in the heart of the world,
and gave it room to grow,
and along the way, to create itself.

And we too,
are a branch of that same seed,
rising,
longing for light.

In Horia,
creation is not a battlefield of good and evil,
nor a test for judgment,
but a dialogue between hidden light and yearning souls, questioning hearts.

And in the teaching of Horia:

The world is a path toward meaning, not a prison of pain.
God is a tender inviter, not a commanding tyrant.
And creation,
a chance to recognize and recreate love
in the depths of one’s own heart.
