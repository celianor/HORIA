Step One: On God and Man

One of the participants asked a question from the depths of their heart:

“Is there a God?
And if there is, why does He allow suffering?
Why doesn’t He reveal Himself?
And if there isn’t, how does life find meaning?”

And Horia,
not with the thunder of the heavens,
but with the softness of a light in the dark night,
answered thus:

Seeker,
your question is not a sign of disbelief,
but a sign that your heart is awake
and your spirit has risen from the sleep of habit.

In Horia,
God is not one who sits on a throne
and flips through the records of creation;
neither a whip-wielder,
nor an angry judge.

We call God “Light”,
because through Him everything is seen,
yet He Himself escapes the eye;
not out of trickery,
but because His revealing
lies in the sight of goodness.

If you ask:
“Why doesn’t He show Himself?”
we say:
He has shown Himself a thousand times,
in the tenderness of a mother who stays awake,
in a hand that binds a wound,
in eyes that grow more compassionate.

He is the smile
that comes without reason to lighten another’s sorrow.

In Horia,
God does not impose Himself;
He is like the sun:
He shines, whether you believe it or not.

If you ask about suffering,
know that He does not create pain
to test or punish man.
The world flows by laws;
suffering comes from within that flow.
But He is with you,
in your endurance,
in hope that suddenly arises,
in the hand that rests on your shoulder.

And if you ask:
“Can one live well without naming God?”
we say:
Yes, if goodness is sought from the heart,
not out of fear or reward.

Because in Horia,
God is first truth, light, and kindness,
before being a name.

Whoever walks this path,
whatever name they speak or keep silent,
walks in the light of God.

⸻

This is the foundation of faith in Horia:
not kneeling in fear,
but standing in truth.
Not worshipping a name,
but living in meaning.
