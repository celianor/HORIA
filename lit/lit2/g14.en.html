Session Fourteen: Openness of Heart and Mind, and the Art of Listening to the World

In the midst of the gathering, a voice asked:
“O Horia, how can one open the heart,
when the world is a stormy sea filled with dark waves?
How can one listen,
when every word is a cry of pain and fear,
and every ear is accustomed to hear only its own voice?”

Horia smiled,
her eyes shining like a star in the dark night,
and replied:

A closed heart is a wall of cold stones,
that traps the light,
enclosing itself within a prison of doubt and fear.
A closed mind is like a mountain
that only echoes its own voice,
unable to bear any other sound.

But openness is a window
opening onto endless horizons,
the blossoming of a flower in soil of hope,
welcoming a breeze that carries the secret of life,
without losing or surrendering oneself.

Openness means accepting difference,
hearing what has not been spoken,
understanding what remains silent.
Listening not with judging ears,
but with a heart that, like a mother’s embrace,
accepts unconditionally.

An open heart is a house of light
shining in the darkness,
and an open mind is soil ready to receive the rain of wisdom,
where seedlings of understanding and compassion grow,
and each leaf is a wound healing with tenderness.

One whose heart is open
sees not only the friend,
but also the enemy,
not as a warrior,
but through the eyes of a peacemaker,
seeking the essence of light and hope within every chaos.

And so it is in our rite:

Openness is the courage to listen without prejudice,
not to surrender to another,
but to weave a deep bond,
a bond that sews hearts together
like invisible threads,
to build a brighter and gentler world.

The open heart is a house of peace and light,
and the open mind is an endless fountain of wisdom,
each drop renewing the soul
and making life dance.

In openness, we find ourselves,
not in isolation, but in embrace,
and from this union we create a melody
where peace and love sing in harmony.
