Religious Superiority and Election: From Prejudice to Dialogue and Empathy

Introduction

In today's world, where religious and cultural diversity is more visible than ever, a correct and balanced understanding of religious concepts such as "election" is of great importance. The belief that a particular group or religion is "chosen" appears in various ways in different religions. Unfortunately, this belief, instead of creating a sense of ethical and spiritual responsibility, often leads to prejudice, hatred, and division among people.

In this article, we will examine the notion of election in religions in a comprehensive and inclusive manner, and explore ways to soften interreligious tensions, strengthen dialogue, and promote global consciousness.

The Concept of Election in Religions

Judaism

In Judaism, the "chosen people" refers to a group tasked with the special mission of preserving and promoting ethical values and justice. This election is a great responsibility, not an inherent privilege or justification to demean others. Unfortunately, misinterpretations and hateful propaganda sometimes distort this concept.

Christianity

Christians believe that faith in Jesus Christ is a path of salvation and love for all humans. Christ's teachings emphasize love for neighbor, forgiveness, and reconciliation. However, some restrictive interpretations can lead to attitudes of superiority.

Islam

Islam teaches that Muslims are charged with maintaining justice, equity, and balance in the world. While the concept of a "just ummah" represents a moral responsibility, sectarian and fanatic interpretations have caused divisions and hatred.

Other Religions

Many other religions also entrust their followers with spiritual missions that, if not accompanied by flexibility and respect, can become sources of division and prejudice.

Social Consequences of Belief in Religious Superiority

When belief in religious superiority is interpreted dogmatically and inflexibly, it leads to violence, the spread of hatred, and lack of trust. This situation prevents dialogue, cooperation, and peaceful coexistence among different communities.

Horia's Solutions to Soften the Climate and Foster Empathy

  • Redefining election as ethical responsibility: Horia views election as a duty of service to humanity, kindness, and justice. All human beings, regardless of religion or origin, share this common responsibility.
  • Promoting intercultural and interreligious dialogue: Dialogue and listening to others are the best ways to eliminate misunderstandings and create empathy.
  • Education and awareness without prejudice: True education about beliefs and cultures, not to create distances but to bring people closer and foster understanding, is a priority for Horia.
  • Encouraging modern and humane interpretation of religious texts: Religious scholars and thinkers should interpret teachings in a human and universal sense.

Global Consciousness and Peaceful Coexistence

Horia believes in a "global consciousness," a common awareness that transcends religious and ethnic boundaries and belongs to all humanity. This consciousness invites us to accept others, respect them, and collaborate to build a better world for all.

Conclusion

Belief in election, when accompanied by prejudice, leads to hatred and violence. But when seen as an ethical responsibility, it can open the way to cooperation, empathy, and building a more just world. Horia invites all human beings to walk this path of global consciousness together, building a world where respect for differences and mutual love prevail.
