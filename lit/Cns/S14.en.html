Booklet IV : The Hidden Light

Not all lights come from the sun;
some rise from within the heart of darkness.

1.
Every sorrow is a lantern —
if you don't rush to put it out,
it might reveal a path
you hadn’t seen before.
2.
What you called “failure”
may have been a door
to a place not of your wishes,
but of your deepest needs.
3.
In the darkest hour of night,
the seed of dawn awakens
not through noise,
but through patience.
4.
Loss sometimes makes room
for what was long forgotten.
Through letting go,
you make space
for what has no name yet.
5.
The wounded absorb light more fully —
like a cracked window
that lets sunlight in
through every break.
6.
Don’t hush sorrow too quickly;
at times, it’s a messenger
from the deepest layers of your soul.
7.
Silence has its own light —
not the one that dazzles the eyes,
but the one that softens the heart.
8.
In getting lost,
you shed what you thought you were
and find what you truly are.
9.
In the Horia path,
darkness is not the enemy —
it is a womb,
the cradle of transformation.
10.
One who lives only in the light
misses the stars.
11.
No tear is ever wasted
if it leads you
back to your own spring.
12.
And know, O watcher of night,
that true lights are hidden —
and only those
who have made peace with darkness
can see them.
