Book I: At the Dawn of the Soul

For the beginning of all things lies in silence, and every awakening comes from a deeper dream.
1.
O newly breathed soul, rising from the horizon like the breath of dawn,
You are still untouched by lies, unbroken by the echoes of the past.
Now, the world asks you: "What do you wish to become?"
And you may choose to remain silent, or to sing your song.
2.
Life is another name for opportunity;
A chance to be—not as others wish you to be,
But as your soul whispers in secret.
3.
Dawn is not a time of day, but a state of soul.
Whoever rises again and lays despair aside has seen the dawn—
Even in the midst of their own midnight.
4.
Do not let the world draw its map upon you
Before you have sketched your own dreams.
For then, you will no longer be yourself, but a copy among many.
5.
Before you seek to change the world, sit and ask yourself:
"Have I passed through silence? Have I found my own voice?"
Without this, all change is surface, and at its core, repetition.
6.
Test yourself—not to judge, but to discover.
Your heart is a mirror that may reveal the unseen,
If you wipe away the dust of habit.
7.
Make peace with yourself before going to war with the world.
For many warriors have drawn their swords
Unaware that the enemy was within.
8.
At the dawn of the soul, truth is simpler:
Wisdom is a smile. Faith, a step. Love, nameless.
Yes, words have not yet veiled the face of meaning.
9.
See each day as the first day of creation.
Such a gaze will renew the world,
And free you from the chains of repetition.
10.
The beginning of the path lies within;
The outer world is but a reflection of the soul’s journey.
Whoever knows themselves holds all the maps.
11.
The child within you still lives, if you let it speak.
It asks about stars, not profit or loss.
It walks the path by longing, not by command.
12.
And know this, O seeker of light: dawn never ends
If you offer your soul to clarity each day.
The sun is not in the sky—it is in your gaze.
And if it shines, the world will shine as well.
