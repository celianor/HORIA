Chapter 4: Beyond Sound

Where words fall silent, truth begins.

1.
The more you speak, the farther you drift from yourself.
Words have wings but no home.
Silence is the home that brings you back to you.
2.
One who truly listens
hears a song in every silence—
in the unfinished sob, the swallowed smile,
in a silence fuller than sound.
3.
Speech is a flawed mirror.
Only silence reflects your whole face—
unadorned, unmasked.
4.
Many have loved you
but could never say it.
And many have said “I love you”
without love in their voice.
5.
A world lies hidden between two words.
And only by listening to that world
can one hear the truth.
6.
A scream is the mask of impatience;
silence is the tongue of trust.
And you—if you dare to be silent—
may hear the world beyond its noise.
7.
A child, before speaking,
speaks the truth with their eyes.
And the mystic, after saying all,
returns to silence, like the child.
8.
Where words fail,
a touch, a glance, a sigh or a tear opens the way.
So be close—sometimes without speech.
9.
Words are not your friends
unless they rise from within.
Otherwise, they are just a mask
upon the face of your silence.
10.
If you want to know someone,
listen to what they don’t say.
There stands truth, without veil.
11.
Silence preaches nothing—
yet many souls have blossomed
in a speechless solitude.
12.
In our path, sound is not sacred;
but the silence from which it rises,
and to which it returns.
