Booklet II: In the Midst of Suffering

Praise of Resilience, Meaning, and Transformation from the Heart of Darkness

Home Back

  1. You are on the path and weary,

    Suffering is not your enemy;

    It is a clear mirror,

    To better know yourself.

    It is sent from the depths of your soul,

    To bring you back to your own home.

  2. Suffering is a light in the night of the soul;

    If you turn away, you remain lost in darkness,

    But if you gaze upon it, its brightness shines upon you,

    Not to burn, but to illuminate.

  3. Resilience is like a tree that in fierce winds,

    Does not break or bend;

    Its roots grow deeper in the hard soil of life,

    Becoming stronger and steadier.

  4. O heart, do not fear breaking.

    He who breaks you will rebuild you,

    And he who rebuilds will make you no longer as before;

    But like steel in fire, harder, brighter, and more precious.

  5. Suffering is neither a heavy burden,

    Nor an incurable wound;

    It is an invitation to depth and silence,

    To reflection and understanding.

    Suffering is an unwelcome but truthful messenger of the inner self.

  6. Meaning is not born in comfort,

    But in the rough waters,

    Among waves of bitterness and hardship,

    The lotus flower blooms.

  7. Shared suffering is a bridge over separations,

    That unites hearts,

    And turns loneliness into compassion;

    A bridge that draws us closer.

  8. Companion on the path, if sometimes the world forgets you,

    Remember you have also built the world,

    And if a structure falls,

    Perhaps it is time to rise, pillarless but truthful.

  9. Remember, after every dark night, a new day dawns;

    Be patient and nurture hope in your heart,

    For life is always flowing, fresh and renewed,

    And the sun will shine again.

  10. Resilience is not mere resistance,

    But dancing amid storms,

    A melody of conscious life,

    That cries out: I am still alive.

  11. Sufferings are the polishers of the soul,

    Each blow removes rusty layers of confusion,

    And brings us closer to purity.

  12. Suffering is not meaningless.

    Meaninglessness is that suffering left unanswered.

    But if you ask, if you stand, if you let it speak,

    It may open you, not break you.
