Book III: In the Luminous Nights

Sometimes, darkness is brighter—if your eyes are free from the light of habit.

1.
Night is not an end;
it is a beginning unseen by eyes, yet felt by the heart.
In the night, the world hides nothing from you — rather, you become more bare to yourself.
2.
When all are asleep, and you remain awake,
you will hear the footsteps of silence.
It has not come to frighten you, but to take your hand and guide you into your depths.
3.
Sleep is not a sibling of night;
sleep is escape from night.
And the one who remains awake, with all the sorrow and brightness of the dark,
it is they who grow.
4.
Light does not always shout.
Sometimes it spreads like a breath,
in the mist, in the eye of a tear, beside the shadow.
5.
If night comes to you, do not hide.
Let it sit, lay its head upon your shoulder,
and ask: “Are you still afraid of yourself?”
6.
I have seen many worship the day,
but ignore the night.
Not knowing that the seed of the sun is planted in the blackness of night;
like a grain that longs to sprout in dark soil.
7.
Sometimes what you desire does not come.
Not from cruelty, but from a knowledge you do not yet possess.
And night is the silence needed before the answer.
8.
Do not fear being lost.
In starless nights,
even if you see nothing,
a new star is born,
if you do not stay in place,
if, like a river, you seek your own path.
9.
Darkness is a frameless mirror.
If you can bear to look into it,
you will see yourself, without veil.
10.
O traveler, salvation will not always come by the road.
Sometimes, you must sit in the midst of fear,
and slowly, gently, tame it.
11.
Night is not the time for dreaming;
it is the time to live without decoration.
If you have the courage to remain silent,
you will find your dream in waking.
12.
And know, O child of luminous nights,
that darkness is not the enemy of light;
it is its teacher.
Without it, no light has meaning.
Darkness is the womb where light is nurtured;
like a child in the belly of the night.
